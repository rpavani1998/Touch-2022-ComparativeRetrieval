Is the Disney Dining Plan Worth It?

Friday, April 24, 2009 Ahhh, Disney dining, it's a love/hate thing for most people, although I'd have to say more people love it than not and I know for my family, it's the one thing we really look forward to.

Since my children are only 6, 3 and 2, character dining is a MUST. On a typical day we usually have 1-2 table service meals scheduled. That sounds like a lot but we LOVE going to breakfasts and finishing up with wonderful dinners. That being said, is it really beneficial for us to purchase the dining plan?

The 2009 Dining Plan

Last year we got the basic plan and to be honest, I think it was waste. Like I said, we prefer breakfasts. The kids are usually the best behaved at breakfast because it's the start of the day and breakfast food is pretty ordinary so even my pickiest eater loves it. With that being said, breakfast is usually the cheapest meal of the day so when we use the dining plan and schedule a lot of breakfasts, we aren'treally getting our money's worth.

Gratuity for the table service meals is not covered in the dining plan and neither are appetizers so if you want to order an appetizer, you pay for it out of pocket. What is included is a soft drink and dessert and this is great IF you don't want wine, beer or a mixed drink with your dinnerand you usually order dessert.

If you have children under the age of 3 they can share a meal with someone or you can order them their own but again, you pay out of pocket for that meal. So, what happened with us last trip was that every meal we were still paying for something, a child's meal, an appetizer, a drink and/or the gratuity so it didn't really feel as though we were getting a deal.

Also, the basic plan allows for a snack every day. Not all of us had a snack every day so by the end of our trip we had all sorts of credits left over for snacks. We of course cashed them in and had plenty of snacks for the trip home!

Now, you can use these meals as you want. If you want to use 3 quick service meals in one day, you can - it doesn't matter how you use them but make sure you use them! Some table service meals require 2 credits. Last year we attended The Spirit of Aloha luau (which was awesome by the way!) and that took up 2 table service credits. You can read about thatHere and Here

In the end, it really depends on you and who you're vacationing with. Even though I said never again, we did book this upcoming vacation to include the basic dining plan. I figure it deserves another chance before I definitely turn my nose up at it.

For more information on the dining plan and the breakdown (math wise) check out:Disney's Dining Plan (That's My Opinion) by Steve Russo

UPDATE: I wrote about our experience using the DDP again in 2009 >>Is the Disney Dining Plan Worth It - Part 2
 
Now that we are DVC members, we do not feel the need to use the DDP. However, since this post was written, many changes have been made. Please see: DDP updates for 2012

Disney's Online Resort Check-in

Tuesday, April 21, 2009 Guess what I just did? I just checked into the Polynesian Resort! No, we're not there yet but Disney allows resort guests to check in up to 10 days prior to their arrival date!

We booked our vacation with AAA - my travel agent's name is Travis Flexer and he is wonderful. He told me I'd receive information in the mail regarding online check in and I did! Checking in online is simple. If you decide to check in online, you will need the following information:
  • Reservation Number
  • Name
  • Arrival and departure dates
  • Your address and phone number
  • A phone number you can be reached at while traveling
  • Credit Card information, if you plan on having a credit card set up for extra room charges, such as internet usage, etc...
  • Special requests, such as room refrigerator or pack n play, etc...
A welcome folder with your Key to the World Card will be waiting for you at the designated Online Check-in area at the resort. You will need to present photo ID to pick up the packet and complete your check-in. Simple!

A few things you should know:
  • You will not be able to change your reservation via the Online Check-in Service.
  • Utilizing Online Check-in will not get your room ready any faster than normal processes.
  • Disneyland is currently not participating in this service.
This service began this year and so we haven't used it until now. I'll let you know how things go! I can say that the online part was easy! Share |

Disney Vacations for 5 or More

Sunday, April 19, 2009 At this moment, we are a family of 5 - me, CJ, and the 3 kids ages 2, 3 and 6. In my experience, this is the maximum amount of people for most rooms at any of the Disney resorts. For example, we LOVE thePolynesian Resort - it's our favorite!
The rooms we get have 2 Queen size beds and the sofa opens up into a day bed, so that is perfect for 5. This year, even though 6 of us are going (my mother in law is going to be with us) we're still okay because my youngest is only 2 and can still sleep comfortably in a pack n play. Here is what the room looks like:

If we decide to go next year, or any year after that for that matter, Wyatt will no longer be free and he will no longer be able to sleep in a pack n play. We can still stay at the Polynesian if Fawn (my MIL) doesn't come along - but of course, we love having her along. That being said, we have already begun looking into other options. Of course there are bigger rooms st the Polynesian, for $900+ a night which is definitely NOT in our budget! We could book 2 rooms at the Polynesian, but the 2 rooms would equal close to over $800 a night anyway, so that is out!

Other options I have looked at so far:

Disney Villas

My first choice is the newBay Lake Tower Villas because, well, they are new and the are right by the monorail - I love being near Disney's monorail transportation! Most villas are for 4 or 5 people, the next one up is for a maximum of 9 people and that is a 2 bedroom villa at $630/night! About $4,000 just for the room??? OUCH!

My second choice would be Disney'sAnimal Kingdom Lodge Villas - Savana View of course. Again, most one bedroom villas are for 4-5 people maximum, then it jumps to a 2 bedroom villa for a maximum of 9 people. For a two-bedroom villa with a standard view of the savanna there is an additional adult charge of $25 per adult per night for more than 2 adults - this villa is $700/night!

So, onto the moderate resorts, looking at the suites. So far, the only one that appeals to me is Disney'sCoronado Springs Resort and a suite there, which allows a maximum of 6 people is $655 a night!

My BFF and her family are staying there this May - I am interested in hearing how they like this resort.

Finally, one last option is the family suites at Disney's All Star Music which is a value resort.

The suites give you all of this for a maximum of 6 people:
  • Queen-size bed
  • Double-size sleeper sofa
  • 2 twin-size sleeper chairs
  • Living room
  • 2 bathrooms
  • Mini-kitchen
  • Vanity area with sink
  • Table and chairs
  • All rooms are non-smoking
  • In-room wall safe
  • Voice mail
  • Internet access (additional fee)
  • Microwave
  • Iron and ironing board
  • Hairdryer
  • Pack 'n Play playard is available upon request
  • Wheelchair-accessible rooms are available
  • Views of the parking lot, courtyard or pool
The price? $184 /night! WOW - why so cheap? Well, first of all, the location. It's near Animal Kingdom and that's about it. You take a bus to get around. This means you need to plan for extra travel time. It's also a loud and crowded resort, of course - it's cheap. Regular rooms start at around $82/night. Lots of times groups such as school trips, cheeerleading camps, etc...stay there.
Honestly, I'd stay there if I REALLY wanted to go to WDW and was on a very tight budget. The layout seems okay:

More Pictures

So, that's where I'm at with this. Staying off property in a rental house, while cost effective, is not something I'd want to do. Staying on site at a Disney resort is one of the main reasons I love to go to Disney! Share |

Best Places to Watch Fireworks in Disney World

Friday, April 17, 2009 On our last trip we didn't get to see fireworks while we were in a park. We were always back in our room by 8 pm or so and that sucked. We never got to see Illuminations, we never got to Hollywood Studios so we didn't see fireworks there and we didn't get to stay at the Magic Kingdom long enough to see Wishes.

We did, however, get to watch Wishes from out resort, the Polynesian - right there on the beach - and that was fantastic!

This year I want to be prepared, so I did a search to find the best places to watch the fireworks and here's what I found:

Magic Kingdom



  • From the Dumbo ride....preferably on Dumbo
  • Outside The Plaza restaurant
  • In front of Splash Mountain, there's a opening you can see them clearly
  • Tomorrowland.... on the bridge...
  • Walkway between the Central Hub in front of the Castle and Tomorrowland
  • The rose garden if you can get there
  • On one of the benches by the riverboat on the Rivers of America
  • Bridge from the central hub to Tomorrow Land
  • From the gallows at Liberty Square
  • On Main Street, but not at the hub; further up the street by the ice cream parlor. You are able to enjoy the 360 degree fireworks and get great photo and video shots! Try to stand where in the first row after the street path the cast members tape off that way people's heads aren't directly in front of you
  • The Grand One Yacht on Bay Lake!
  • From the monorail as it circles the Seven Seas Lagoon. Great views of the entire fireworks display
  • The Beach at one of the Magic Kingdom resorts (Polynesian, Grand Floridan, Wilderness Lodge, Ft. Wilderness or Contemporary)!

Hollywood Studios


  • From the courtyard in front of the Mickey's hat.
  • From Hollywood Blvd. sitting at one of the umbrella tables.
  • Standing right in front of the Crossroads.


Epcot


  • On the bridge by Mexico
  • Near American Adventure
  • From France
  • In Germany
  • In front of the Norway, good view, all facilities within easy reach (Kringla Bakeri, restrooms, water fountain)
  • Near Japan. The view by the Tori Gate is great
  • Between Norway and China there is a cast member entrance set back a ways from the walkway. You can step back into this area, get out of the way of the traffic and have a virtually unobstructed view of the lagoon and the sky
  • Anywhere around the lagoon
  • Anywhere around the World Showcase
(courtesy of allears.net)

This year we have a Wishes Cruise scheduled - I am so so excited for this! However, we are also going to try and seeIlluminations one night and I would really love to be in the Magic Kingdom to seeWishes at least once on this upcoming trip!

Disney's Dining Plan

Tuesday, April 14, 2009 Last trip, after all was said and done the one thing I said I wouldn't do again was the dining plan. However, probably due to laziness, when we booked this year's vacation I did include the dining plan.

Here's a breakdown of the cost for the dining plan in 2009, what the terms mean and which package includes what:


Quick Service Dining Plan
Adult: $29.99
Child: $8.99

Dining Plan
Adult: $39.99
Child: $10.99

Deluxe Dining Plan
Adult: $71.99
Child: $20.99

Premium Dining Plan
Adult: $159.00
Child: $109.00

Platinum Dining Plan
Adult: $209.00
Child: $144.00

There is also a Wine and Dine add-on package for an additional $39.99 per night. This package entitles you to one bottle of wine per night, redeemable at table service restaurants during your stay.

Here's a mini glossary:
  • Quick Service Meal – a meal from a counter service establishment like Pecos Bill’s in the Magic Kingdom or the Electric Umbrella at Epcot. Within the Dining Plans, this meal would include an entrée, dessert and a soft drink.
  • Table Service Meal – a meal from a traditional restaurant or a buffet. This meal also includes entrée, dessert and soft drink. Beware; certain restaurants require two table service credits for a single meal. An example would be the California Grill at the Contemporary.
  • Snacks – may typically be purchased from counter service locations, kiosks or carts throughout Walt Disney World parks and resorts. Some examples of eligible snack options would include: a frozen ice-cream, a fruit bar, a bag of chips, a 20 oz. bottle of Coke or a 20 oz. bottle of water.
  • Refillable Drink Mug –is available, for cash, from each Disney World resort. This year, the price is $12.49 and once purchased you can get free refills at identified soft drink locationsat your resort for the length of your stay.
Now, just what do you get with each plan? Here's a breakdown:

Quick Service Dining Plan

The Disney Quick Service Dining Plan is the newest plan and includes two quick service meals and two snacks per person, per night of your package stay for everyone in the party ages 3 and over. The plan also includes one refillable drink mug per person.

Dining Plan

The Disney Dining Plan includes one quick service meal, one snack, and one table service meal per person, per night of your package stay for everyone in the party ages 3 and over.

Deluxe Dining Plan

The Disney Deluxe Dining Plan includes three table service meals and two snacks per person, per night of your package stay for everyone in the party ages 3 and over. The plan also includes one refill¬able drink mug per person.

Premium Dining Plan

The Disney Premium Plan offers a complement of dining, recreation, and entertainment options. Each guest on the Disney Premium Plan receives one table service breakfast, lunch and dinner and two snacks per day. The plan also includes one refillable drink mug per person. In addition to these dining options, this plan also provides the following entertainment opportunities: preferred fireworks viewing for Wishes and Illuminations; unlimited golf, boat rental, fishing excursions; admission to Cirque du Soleil’s show, La Nouba; access to Disney Children’s Activities Centers; admission to a number of Disney’s theme park tours.

Platinum Dining Plan

This package includes everything in the Premium package plus: personalized pre-arrival itinerary planning; a fireworks cruise; private in-room babysitting; nightly turn down service; a spa Treatment; and a Disney Photopass CD.

Tips for Packing for Your Disney Vacation!

Wednesday, April 8, 2009 Many people travel by plane to get to Disney. We considered flying this trip but have decided to drive. However, when we thought we'd be flying I panicked! HOW was I going to pack everything I needed for 5 people?! Diapers, clothing, bathroom stuff, bathing suits, towels, etc..

Here is what I was going to do and if you are flying and feel overwhelmed with packing, this may help:

  • Pool gear - only pack suits - forget beach towels. The resorts have a little shed by the pool that has towels in it. If yours doesn't, take a room towel. Beach towels take up too much room if you try to pack them!
  • Send yourself a package - If you need to take diapers, formula, extra snacks, etc... box it up and send it UPS or FedEx! Plan for it to arrive the day before or the day of your arrival. The resort will receive your package and hold it for you. Yes, you will pay to ship the package but it's worth it to avoid the stress and hassle of taking all that stuff with you - don't forget about the luggage fees now too!
  • Order Groceries - Don't want to ship stuff? Create an account online with Garden Grocer and place your order before you get to your resort. You can schedule a delivery and your order will be delivered to your resort or sometimes even your room! This saves you from having to pack all kinds of extra diapers, water, snacks for the kids, etc.This is also great for those who have special diet needs!
These tips will save you so much time and you'll be able to enjoy your vacation more because you won't have to worry about these details!

When it comes to souvenirs you may find yourself wondering how to get what you buy back to your resort. If you're at a theme park and get lots of stuff or maybe something that is just too big to carry around the rest of the day, you can have the item sent to your resort for you. You can also have the resort ship it to your home so you don't have to worry about how to get everything home! However, remember, there are fees involved for this.

Since we're driving we don't need to be so concerned about packing but the Garden Grocer may come in good use, for diapers and such. Share |

Disney Vacation Planning With Kids

Monday, April 6, 2009 No matter how excited you may be to take that trip to Disney, if you travel with children, keep this in mind:It IS all about them
There are so many things to see and do in WDW as an adult but if you are going with kids remember to plan your trip around them. This means you may not get to see and do everything thatyou hope to but believe me, that is much better to deal with than unhappy children!

On my family's last trip, we didn't keep the kids in mind as well as we should have. Basically, I did all the planning and I was trying to find things that would keep everyone happy. From my 85 year old grandma, to my mom and MIL, to CJ and then the kids. I wanted everyone to see and do the stuff they wanted - I wanted us all to have a great time! It didn't work out so great.

Here are a few tips, that may seem like common sense but it's surprising how many people don't think of them!
  • Schedule down time - yes, PLAN for it. Don't run yourself and the kids all over the place from dusk 'til dawn. If part of your rest time includes relaxing by a resort pool, be prepared to have nap time after that! Kids become very tired swimming even for only an hour.
  • Plan meals EARLY! - So many people say breakfasts are a waste - especially if you have the meal plan. 'Get the most for your money, have dinners planned at expensive places.' That's great if you don't have kids! Breakfasts are excellent meals to schedule if you have children. The kids are usually in good spirits because it's the beginning of the day. If you do want to have a dinner - make reservations early! Between 4-5:30 is a good time. Any later than that and kids may be over tired and overstimulated from the day's events.
  • Decide which rides and attractions you want to see BEFORE you go - This is another obvious one but so many people, including us on our last last trip, get to a park and then stand there looking at the park map, trying to decide where to go first. This wastes time (DUH) and the kids may be overly anxious to go and see/do things! Don't make them wait any longer than they may have to.
  • Many resorts have special kid events planned. Epcot has KIDCOT - These Fun Stops are "Kid Stations" located in several Future World pavilions and in all of the World Showcase countries. These areas, staffed by Cast Members, feature an activity and/or craft table especially for children.
  • For more tips and information, visit Planning Disney With Kids
This time, we already have it set in our minds, this trip is all about the kids. Not to say we won't get to see and do things we as adults will love, but we will definitely have things planned out much better! Share |

Tower of Terror

Photo courtesy of wdwinfo.com

The one ride I can't wait to experience again and the one ride I will most likely being experiencing alone,The Twilight Zone Tower of Terror at Disney's Hollywood Studios.

This ride is a free fall type ride but that isn't even half of the reason why I love it! Yes, the thrill of being dropped down, then pulled back up and dropped again, or which ever order the Tower decides to send you in, is super exciting. I especially love when they open the doors to the building and you can see the park for a brief moment as you're falling and screaming!

The entire experience is exciting, from the moment you step into the Hotel Lobby - everything is covered by dust and cobwebs. There's a set of leather chairs which I found out, are genuine Renaissance antiques!
Then you go to the Library. The door closes and the lights dim. As the room gets dark, the TV turns on and there in black and white is Rod Serling! He gives this unique introduction for that night's episode.

Once the intro is over The Hollywood Tower Hotel appears around you as it was in it's prime. Then you see a young couple, a little girl and a bellhop enter the elevator. The doors close, they start to go up, lightning hits the building and they vanish.

Suddenly the lights in the Library turn back on, a door opens and you enter the "Twilight Zone." Now you're in the boiler room of the hotel waiting to board a 1917 service elevator. This is where the ride begins! You get in your seat on the elevator and strap yourself in! The doors close and the elevator heads up. First stop, the doors open and you see a very cool optical illusion. The doors close again and at the next stop, you move forward.

This ride is exhilirating from beginning to end...and a bit spooky!
