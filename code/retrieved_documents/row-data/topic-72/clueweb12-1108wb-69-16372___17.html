Learn Chinese Words

Chinese Language

Chinese words, phrases, and sentences are formed by Chinese characters. In addition to learn Chinese characters, you also need to learn Chinese words, phrases, and sentences.

Chinese words, phrases, and sentences are formed by Chinese characters. Chinese characters are called the founding blocks of Chinese language. Radicals are the component of Chinese characters while strokes are the basic elements of radicals.

KidsChinesePodcast.com is dedicated to helping you expand your Chinese vocabulary.

Like learning other languages, there will come a time when you will need to learn Chinese words as basic vocabulary. There are numerous ways to start studying Chinese words. As a beginner, you may have a lot of questions on Chinese words.

How do you know the order to learn Chinese words?

Usually, there are three main ways to help you decide the order. They are frequency of use, complexity of the written language and situation. Each way has pros and cons.

1. Learn Chinese words by frequency of their use

One way to learn Chinese words is by frequency of their use. Like any other languages, you will find there are some terms which get used more frequently. Other expressions will be used only rarely. Many language scholars think if you pick up the 20% most commonly used terms in Mandarin Chinese, then you will be able to understand 80% of what you hear and read. This is a good news advice for you to learn Chinese words! However, the downside of this tactic is that you will find grammatical terms often come up more frequently than actual nouns. For example, you will see “and” and “the” come up frequently in English sentences.

Learn Chinese Language

Chinese Language

For most of English Speakers, the Chinese language seems to be a complex mystery intimidating to unravel and to understand. However, to learn Chinese language is not difficult as you thought because the grammar of Chinese language is much simpler than English. China has over five thousand years history, so the Chinese language contains the rich Chinese culture. If you wish to learn Chinese, we suggest you start learning to speak Chinese first just like a little kid. You will be amazed how fast you can speak Chinese!

If you are interested in the history of the Chinese language, let’s take a quick look at it. Historically, the Chinese language evolved from Proto-Sino-Tibetan and has ascended into modern day with most Chinese communities speaking either Mandarin or Cantonese forms of the Chinese language while Mandarin is the official Chinese language. There are different varieties on both of these depending on the geographical localities. The linguistics of the Chinese language are easier to trace back in time than are the characters used for Chinese alphabet, as there is rare documentation found to support the origins.

Chinese Podcast: A Great Way to Learn Chinese

Chinese Language

What is Podcast?

A podcast is a series of digital audio or video file that are released episodically and often it’s downloaded through internet. The word replaced webcast in common use with the success of the iPod (one of the most popular portable MP3 players) and its role in the rising popularity and innovation of web feeds.

You do not have to have an iPod to listen to a podcast. In fact, there are many other MP3 players that are good for listening to podcasts. You can listen to podcasts on anything that plays MP3 files. Once the podcast is downloaded, it’s just an MP3.You can listen to it using a computer or, more commonly, to a portable MP3 player. At the core a podcast is an audio file that is automatically received from the internet and then synced to your MP3 player.

You can listen to a podcast exactly what you want, when you want and how you want! With podcasts, you’re in control. You can rewind a podcast. You can play it over and over. You can pause it, store it wherever you want. You can delete it when you want. Podcasts give you the control. You can listen to podcasts on your computer, burn them to CD or transfer them to your MP3 player. You’re in control of when and how you listen.

How to Learn Chinese to Achieve the Best Result

Newsletter

Kids Chinese Podcast: Newsletter No.1

Dear subscribers,

Welcome to Chinese Lessons at KidsChinesePodcast.com!

Chinese is the one of the most spoken language in the world. Mandarin Chinese language is the official language of China and Taiwan, as well as one of four official languages of Singapore, and one of six official languages of the United Nations. Kids Chinese Podcast teaches Mandarin Chinese.

Congratulations on starting your journey to learn Chinese!

How to learn Chinese to achieve the best learning result?

Because different person has different learning style, the answer to the question may be different. We list our research result and suggestion as the following to help you learning Chinese and we will start the learning Chinese journey with you, together, we will explore and improve our Chinese lessons to help you achieve the best learning result!

Kids Chinese Podcast is designed for kids from 5 years old up and adult Chinese learners to learn Mandarin Chinese. This Chinese course helps you to learn Chinese listening and speaking via podcast, and Chinese reading and writing via podcast transcript and exercise worksheet. Lessons’ topics are designed using practical dialogues starting with focusing on kids’ daily life in the United States while you receive systematic training on Chinese listening, speaking, reading and writing.

We recommend the following steps to achieve the best Chinese learning results.
1. Language Study Research shows that using auditory and visual media at same time can achieve better learning results. Sign up and become a registered member. After you sign up, you will get access to all transcript and PDF worksheet. Have a transcript in front of you when listening to the lesson.

2. Start early as possible as you can. When children acquire their mother tongue, they understand and speak before they are capable of reading and writing. This is also true when learning a foreign language. In the Level-1 lessons, our goal is to let students have a feeling on Chinese Mandarin’s phonics and Chinese Characters, and build a sense of Chinese language in a short time. Therefore, the transcript serves a very good study guide. Musical tones are perhaps the most difficult thing in Mandarin Chinese, that’s why we use the native-language-acquiring approach, namely, the just listening and then understanding way. Language Research Study reports that kids under five can speak a language without any accent just a native speaker. Kids can be parents’ teacher in terms of speaking and correct pronunciation. Help you child to start learning Chinese early as possible as you can!

3. Listen as many times as possible whenever you have a chance , when you driving your child to school, before bedtime etc.

4. Try to imitate after the teacher, even if the sounds seem to be difficult to pronounce. Kids usually are not shy, adults,don’t be shy!

5. The worksheet of each lesson has a translation exercise for you, so please fill in your answer and then compare your answer to the correct one that the teacher announced at the end of your next lesson.

6. Practice what you have learned whenever you have a chance . That’s why we promote parents and kids learn Chinese together. Parents and kids are the best study pal, this approach motivates parents to set a good role model on discipline and nurture kids a good study habit, in the same time, parents and kids learn, practice speaking Chinese in a really fun and effective way.

7. Encourage good study habits by scheduling regular Chinese study time. For example, everyday, after dinner, spend about 20 minutes. Firstly, listen the audio lesson about 10 minutes, then do the worksheet to practice reading and writing for about 5 minutes then spend another 5 minutes, practice speaking. Parents and kids can talk using what we have learned in the lesson and use what we have learned in the previous lessons.

8. Be persistent and never give up! Unlike many Western languages which share some common linguistic roots with English and which can often be learned fairly quickly with a large amount of self-study for adults, acquiring a basic competency in a language as different from English as Chinese requires a high level of commitment and persistence. If parents can be persist, your child will be too!

9. One of the best ways parents can help with your child’s educationis to take part yourself. Sign up for the Chinese lessons and learn Chinese together with your child.

Parents and kids listen to Kids Chinese Podcast together and learn Chinese together, having fun and achieve the best learning results.
