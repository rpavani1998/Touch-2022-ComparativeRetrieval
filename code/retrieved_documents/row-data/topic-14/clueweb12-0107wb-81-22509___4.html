CAN ACUPUNCTURE AND CHINESE MEDICINE HELP YOU?

BY DR YUN-FEI LU




In Australia, more and more people are seeking alternative natural healing methods for their health, and wonder whether their health problems can be treated by acupuncture and Chinese herbal medicine. Acupuncture and Chinese medicine (an ancient medical system) are growing in popularity around the world, despite the technological advancements of conventional western medicine.

While western medicine is fulfilling essential roles for the treatment of many illnesses, there are still so many health problems which are not satisfactorily treated. Two typical examples are the treatment of the age related degenerative health problems and the treatment of cancer. Chinese medicine offers a holistic approach to decrease the rate of age related physiological decline of the human body. In China, most cancer patients are using some kind of treatments from traditional Chinese medicine.


WHY DO I NEED ACUPUNCTURE AND CHINESE MEDICINE?

While in China it is a tradition for people to seek Chinese medicine treatments, here in our western society people seek Chinese medicine treatments because of various reasons. From my clinical experience, the main reasons can be summarized below.

- "Western medicine can not help me any more." Some cancer patients in western medical treatment programs, are told that nothing further can be done for them. Including many other medical problems, people realizing that they have to continue their medication for lifetime to control the health problem and in seeking an alternative method, they turn to Chinese medicine.

- "I am suffering the side effects from western medical treatment." This is often heard from people using western medicines, who worry about long term damage to their body. For example, HRT (hormonal replacement treatment) has been used as an effective treatment for menopause syndrome, but there are concerns of its side effects.

- "I know that acupuncture and Chinese medicine are more effective for my current health problem." These people have usually already experienced Chinese medicine treatments or know the effectiveness of Chinese medicine from other media sources. For example, acupuncture is effective for pain relief in many acute and chronic illnesses, such as lower-back pain, frozen shoulder, and many other muscular problems. Acupuncture has also been shown to be effective for the treatment of hemiplegia, due to strokes. Herbal medicine is effective for the treatment for depression, stress, and some gynecological problems, such as period pain.

- "My GP recommended I see a Chinese medical doctor." As acupuncture and Chinese herbal medicine are more and more accepted in western society, GPs are starting to refer patients to seek Chinese medicine as complementary treatments. An integrated treatment of western medicine and traditional medicine can be an effective method to enhance the treatment result and reduce side effects. Treatment of cancer is one typical case.

- "I personally am very keen on natural and alternative medicine." Some people prefer to use natural methods like acupuncture and Chinese herbal medicine to maintain their health and to treat ailments when necessary.


WHAT KIND OF HEALTH PROBLEMS ARE EFFECTIVELY TREATED BY ACUPUNCTURE AND CHINESE MEDICINE?

The World Health Organization (WHO) has recognized acupuncture for the treatment of over 200 medical conditions. The following are some examples, of common conditions which benefit from acupuncture and Chinese medicine, based on my clinical experience.

Muscle & joints: Muscular pain in neck, shoulder, back, arms and legs - such as arthritis, neuralgia, back pain, tendonitis, stiff neck, stroke, cerebral palsy, sprains, painful heels;
Neurology: Migraine, sciatica, headache;
Mental-Emotional: Anxiety, depression, stress, insomnia, sleep disorders, chronic fatigue, smoking, and drug addictions;
Gynaecology & Reproductive: Infertility, miscarriage, morning sickness, menstrual disorders (irregular periods or cramps), menopause syndrome, pre-menstrual syndrome (PMS), urinary disorder;
Cardiovascular-circulation: Post stroke symptoms, heart palpitation, high blood pressure;
Respiratory: Flu, cough, asthma;
Immunology: Allergies, disorders of immune system, complementary treatment for cancers;
Gastro-intestinal: Irritable bowel syndrome (IBS), chronic abdominal pain, hyperacidity, ulcers, chronic diarrhea, indigestion, constipation, diabetes, eating disorders, weight problems;
Eyes-Ears-Nose-Throat: Hay fever, dizziness, sore throat, poor hearing, tinnitus, sinus infection;
Skin: Eczema, acne, urticaria, skin dry skin and itchy skin.


PREVENTION OF DISEASES IS AS IMPORTANT AS TREATMENT OF DISEASES

It should be noted that prevention of diseases is one of the most important features in Chinese medicine. Chinese medicine has been helping people to maintain health by using acupuncture and herbal medicine, including a balanced diet, and combining various exercises, like Chi-Gong (Chi Kung) and Tai-Chi. From ancient China, it was said that the best doctor was the one who was able to prevent disease before it occurred rather than to treat the disease after it had manifested. Chinese medicine believes that balance and harmony of the body's energy (Qi or Chi) is the basis of health. Acupuncture and herbal medicine can restore the body's balance before physical illnesses fully develop.
