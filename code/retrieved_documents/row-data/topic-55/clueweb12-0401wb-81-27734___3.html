Physical Therapy Myths

Physical therapy is just for people who have had strokes or surgery.

This is really is more of a misunderstanding than a myth. The scope ofphysical therapy is broad. The profession ofphysical therapy addresses a wide variety of conditions: yes strokes and surgeries… but alsosprains/strains , tendonitis, bursitis, back pain, shoulder pain, TMJ, urinary incontinence, headaches, dizziness , etc.  The concept to understand is that PAIN is just merely a symptom…it is not the problem.  Some underlying problem (dysfunction) with the human body causes the signaling of pain. Physical therapy is designed to identify and handle the underlying problem so that your pain has no reason to exist.  When you handle the problem causing the pain, the pain goes away.  So if you have pain, physical therapycan help.



Medication controls my pain, so I don’t need physical therapy! 

This is a very important myth or misunderstanding to address. The first thing that needs to be understood is PAIN and the role of PAIN.  Pain is a protective mechanism, it simply is a “sign” or “signal” that something is wrong.  Pain prevents us from doing things that we shouldn’t, so we don’t cause more harm to ourselves.  Also, PAIN IS CAUSED by an underlying problem, but not the problem itself.  An common analogy to help us better understand this is the problem of a flat tire.  Now the symptom of the flat tire is no air, but the problem is that there is a hole in the tire.  You can treat the symptom of the flat tire (no air) all you want, but until you fix the problem (the hole) your symptom will continue to exist.   So the fact that you can control your pain with medication can in actuality do you more harm for you are “numbing” your pain signal, and by only treating the pain, you allow the problem causing the pain to remain, giving you future issues.  This leads to many things: frustration, increased costs on medication and physician visits, lost time from work, inability to do the things you enjoy, etc.  Now medication does provide us comfort, so that is good!  The best scenario is to obtain comfort from your pain by using medication while correcting the problem causing the pain; in the end you have no pain and no problem. Physical Therapy is centered around identifying and correcting dysfunctions that cause pain.  If you are having pain, just know that you don’t have to live with it.Contact Peak Physical Therapyand get it handled.  

Physical therapy and chiropractic treatments can’t be done together.

Contrary to belief, this can be one of the most beneficial forms of care; why?? For it brings two specialties together.  The concept to grasp here is...”mobilize and stabilize”.  Chiropractors are known for their great manual and manipulation (“mobilization”) skills of the joints, especially the spine. Physical Therapists, when working in tandem with a chiropractor can then focus intently on the “stabilization” of the spine or joint which involves ensuring that the soft tissue that support the joints and spine (tendons/ligaments/muscles) are adequately flexible/strong to maintain the proper alignment of the joint/spine.  Chiropractic patients will find that with the assistance of physical therapy they “hold” there spinal alignment for longer periods.
