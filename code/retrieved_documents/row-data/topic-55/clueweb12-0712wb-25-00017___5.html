Physical Therapy Kenmore Square

Joint Ventures Physical Therapy and Fitness opened in May of 2005. This Kenmore Square Physical Therapy office was started after Dan Brownridge and Dave Larson spent years working as physical therapists in the Boston area.

About Physical Therapy - Kenmore Square

Dan and Dave recognized that the therapist-patient relationship needed to be the top priority. They wanted all patients to be guaranteed to be seen one-on-one by a licensed physical therapist, not an assistant, aide or student. They knew that this would result in the best possible skilled care and faster functional outcomes.

Dan and Dave also understood that patients were receiving different advice from different professionals. The massage therapist would say something different than the personal trainer and the patient was stuck trying to decide who to trust. Dan and Dave decided to bring all the practitioners under one roof to work together to achieve each individual’s goals. The result is the truly integrated healthcare experience that is Joint Ventures Physical Therapy and Fitness.

Physical Therapy - Kenmore Square - Overview

Individuals generally consult a Physical Therapist for the management of medical problems or other health-related conditions that cause pain, limit the ability to move, or limit the performance of functional activities. Physical Therapists evaluate individuals, diagnose conditions, and develop plans using treatment techniques that promote the ability to move, reduce pain, restore function, and prevent disability.

Whether your condition is neurological or orthopedic, new or old, our aim is to work to heal your injury and improve your fitness level to avoid future injuries. We accomplish this by using our state-of-the-art rehabilitation and fitness equipment, as well as a combination of manual, traditional and sports medicine techniques. All of our Kenmore Square physical therapy sessions are conducted in a comfortable private room where you can ask as many questions as you need to about your condition or treatment. Once you've completed physical therapy, we provide a program that you can follow at home, or we can create a program for you that can be followed at our fitness center.

  • Aquatic Therapy - Aquatic therapy utilizes buoyancy and hydrostatic pressure, as well as variable resistance to create a workout for those who need a non-impact, yet challenging rehabilitation program.
