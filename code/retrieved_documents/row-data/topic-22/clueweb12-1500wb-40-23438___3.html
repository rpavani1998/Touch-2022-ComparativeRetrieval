Subway Switches From Pepsi to Coke

The Wall Street Journal reports (sub. req'd) that Subway is switching its fountain business from Pepsi to Coke. According to the article, Subway has more U.S. and Canadian restaurants than McDonald's, which I was unaware of.

A spokesman for Pepsi said, "It's inconceivable to think this has anything to do with products or service. This is about an incredibly big bag of cash, and we hope Coke is offering the same deal to the rest of its customers."

I'm sure Subway got a good deal from Coke, but I wouldn't say it's inconceivable this decision was made on product quality as well. When deciding which restaurant to eat at, the first question that invariably comes to my mind is, "Do they sell Coke products, or Pepsi products?" This is an extremely important question. If the answer is the latter, then the food simply must beoutstanding, so that I'm willing to put up with Pepsi. It is for this reason that I largely avoid the Pizza Hut, Kentucky Fried Chicken, Taco Bell triumvirate, all formerly owned by PepsiCo, Inc., and still selling that inferior product. If I go somewhere I don't know what they sell, I order a Coke. If the waiter/waitress asks me, "Is Pepsi OK?," I make it a point to give a disappointing look, sometimes settling for water.

Coke simply has better products, and Coca Cola, Co.'s superiority over PepsiCo with respect to their flagship colas is substantially comparable to the superiority the 1927 Yankees would have over the little league team I played on when was 11. (Side note: For purposes of this discussion, and really for purposes of my life, I'm ignoring that Mountain Dew and Mello-Yellow exist.)

On Monday, Motley Fool writer Rex Moore reported the results of a highly scientific blind taste test atFool headquarters between Vanilla Coke and Pepsi Vanilla. The results? Out of 19 taste-testers, 68% chose the correct response, Vanilla Coke. Which only leaves us with one question: What in the world is wrong with the taste buds of 32% ofFool employees? (Note #2: I have never tasted Pepsi Vanilla. I do not believe, however, that in any way hinders my comparison of the two products.)

In sum, I'd like to say congratulations to Subway. You have chosen wisely. And I don't have to avoid you anymore.
