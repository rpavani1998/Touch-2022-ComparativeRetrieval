FAQ

I’ve gotten a lot of questions about food as well as everything else over the years, so I did what anyone else with tons of time on his hands would do: I started an FAQ page.

If you have something you want to know and you think the ol’ FoodFella could help, send it along and maybe it will be on this page.

What’s your favorite restaurant in Toronto?

It’s too hard to pick just one place that I really love. There are so many great places, so the best way to find out if there’s a restaurant that stands out is to look on the reviews page.

What type of food do you enjoy the most?

Again, it’s almost impossible to pick just one. I’d say my favorites include some traditional French (because that’s what I learned), classic American as well as barbecue, Italian, Spanish, Mexican, Seafood, Sushi and Japanese, Thai and contemporary Canadian.

Why do you shop at restaurant supply shops?

I find the quality of the products there to be really good. If something is designed to withstand the abuse it will get in a restaurant kitchen, then chances are it will last almost forever in a household kitchen.

Do you watch the food-related reality shows?

No, I don’t. Mainly because I find that those shows are produced for entertainment purposes rather than showcasing the food itself. They focus on the human struggle and the competition as well as the drama. Although I do occasionally enjoyIron Chef America and Restaurant Makeover.

What’s are your favorite “restaurant cities” ?

Easily Toronto is one of the best, followed by (in no particular order) New York City (providing you stay away from the tourist and celebrity restaurants), Vancouver, Montreal, Buffalo (yes,the Buffalo. Plenty of awesome, non-chain and non-tourist restaurants there… you just have to look) and Chicago. I’m told Ottawa has a growing restaurant scene and I’ve been wanting to visit Philadelphia and Washington DC.

How come TheFoodFella.com isn’t just about food?

There are tons of great food blogs out there – many of which that are far better than mine. So I wanted to do something different. I wanted to share parts of my life, both food and non-food related and add something of a personal touch to it… the same way I do with cooking.

For a stove: gas or electric?

I prefer gas, but I’ve been using a lot of electric as well these days. It’s a personal preference and while many say that gas is better (and I do love it) you can make a fine meal on an electric stove too.

How do I find a good espresso/cappuccino maker?

Go to specialty stores. Try to find a place that sells more than one brand. Ask a lot of questions and try the coffee!

When boiling pasta, do you salt the water?

Yes. I was taught by one of my chefs to “make it taste like the sea”. But that being said, if you’re making a dish that has a lot of salt in it (pasta with prosciutto or pancetta, for example) then I ease up on the salt in the pasta water.

Best food to eat on the TTC?

There is no such thing because nobody should be eating on a bus, streetcar or subway. I can’t think of anything worse than a subway car full of people who have to smell whatever it is some guy (or girl) brought on board with them.

If you could have dinner with one person, who would it be?

If we’re talking “living or dead” then I’m going to have to go with Johnny Carson. If it’s just “living” then that’s an easy answer… my wife.
