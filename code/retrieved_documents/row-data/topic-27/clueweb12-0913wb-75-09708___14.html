I'm Julie, and I live with my husband and three young daughters in New South Wales suburbia, Australia. This is the online journal I kept until recently, of how we are trying to live more simply & sustainably in suburbia.

This blog is on indefinite hiatus but please feel free to look around my archives for some inspiration in your own journey to living more lightly and sustainably.



Wednesday, January 27, 2010

Planned obsolescence...

...makes me want to shout rude words.

This week the switch on the electric kettle broke and the four-year-old microwave stopped working. We duly ring the closest electrical repair centre (a half-hour drive away), to be told "Nah mate, we don't touch kettles, you need an electrician", and "Sounds like your magnetron has gone. Cost ya more to fix it than to buy a new one."

I think we've all been there before.

Doesn't get any less frustrating each time it happens.

Having said that, we only use the microwave for re-heating leftovers, melting butter for baking and heating milk for our daily coffee, plus it takes up a lot of bench space... and I think I have convinced hubby that we really don't need a new one. As for the kettle, I've been looking for an affordable vintage stove-top kettle for ages, so it sounds like it's time to get more intense about that search! It's a pity that they tend to be collectible and therefore expensive though.

In the meantime, does anyone remember Birko food warmers? I was given an old one when I was a Uni student (possibly it was my grandmother's), and I still have it. Eminently useful little gadget and still going strong.



If you are trying to find where to get rid of your broken microwave too, Novacastrians can checkhere for local recycling information, and Aussies can checkhere for more information.


Cheers,
