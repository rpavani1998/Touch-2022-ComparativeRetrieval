Old gas stove

topic posted Wed, October 4, 2006 - 3:52 PM by  Alex

Advertisement

'Kay, I found a beautiful old gas stove/oven for FREEE!!!! Some apartment owner was remodeling, I guess.

Anyway, one of the ones they were throwing out is in terrific shape -- only a couple of chips. However, there's a sticker on it that says that it has no pilot. So, I guess you have to light it with a match every time you use it?

My question is, an older gas stove like this (with no pilot) be "upgraded" with an electronic igniter? I've searched on the internet, but haven't exactly been able to figure out what to use in the search. I did find one antique gas stove refurbisher who said that it's illegal in the state of California to modify this sort of thing. But, hey, I live in Arizona! :D

The flip side of this is: maybe it's actually better to have NO electronic ignition and NO pilot???? Maybe there are some hard-core people who love fewer gadgets (I'm kinda in that camp) who could offer me some advice??? Maybe there really aren't more dangers to using a non-piloted gas stove???

Thanks so much!

    I used to have a vintage 40's Maytag stove and I loved it. Lots of room on top to put things while you were cooking, and it had an oven you could turn off half way through and it retained the heat. Unfortunately, the oven pilot light kept going out, and the idiot who came to fix it ended up irreparably breaking it...banged things with a hammer that shouldn't have been banged, and broke a piece off. Unfortunately, after an extensive local and internet search, I couldn't find the replacement part.

    I ended up buying a new GE stove which I had for a few years and hated it. No room on the top of the stove to put anything. Badly insulated oven...compared with the old Maytag. And the burners were wimpy...didn't get hot enough. The electronic ignition was touchy...wouldn't work if you got the littlest bit of grease on the burner. Most of the time I had to light it by hand.

    Now I have my Mom's vintage 50's Magic Chef. Had to get used to the hotter burners again, but I wouldn't trade it for the world. The oven doesn't have a pilot light so it has to be lit by hand. The burners do have a pilot. If I remember right, you can cap off the pilots to the top burners, but depending on the state you live in it may not be legal.

    At any rate...if you have someone come in to fix the stove, make sure you ask ahead of time if they can repair vintage stoves. The idiot that broke my Maytag wanted to charge me for the service call. Sorry, but I don't pay people for breaking things.

      <I should be able to just disconnect the gas when I'm not expecting to use it, shouldn't I? (What would be the procedure for that, anyway?) >

      if safety is a concern, you should be able to do this either inside or out, the only reason the keep gas running to a stove is to feed the pilot.

      most homes that have a gas line to the kitchen will have a valve to cut the line right before it hits the stove. if you sont have one, you should. its a simple, in-line valve, and depending on where it is installed, you could easily turn off gas to the whole stove when not in use.

      this makes lighting the stove a three step process (1. gas to stove, 2. light a match, 3. gas to burner) but if you can hang with that, its unlikely your boys will ever turn on the gas by mistake. join to post
