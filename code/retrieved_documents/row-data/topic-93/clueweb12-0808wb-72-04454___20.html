When I was growing up in the 1960’s, my dad would always tell jokes. One of his favorites was the water joke which went like this. The bartender went through several different drinks and the gent named each one correctly. So, the bartender sat up a glass of water. The gent drank the water and spat it out immediately. When the bartender asked the gent what it was, the gent replied, “I don’t know, but they’ll never be able to sell it!!”

He didn’t know how wrong he was. The soda pop companies have duped you into buying their “bottled water.” Dasani, from Coca-Cola, Deja Blue from Seven-Up, and Aquafina from Pepsi. I have also done ph tests on the water that comes from these companies, only to find it an acidic 5.5 ph. It is actually dehydrating you. The soda is a 2.7 ph. The body needs 7.365 ph in order to operate holistically. Anything less than that creates illness and disease that could otherwise be avoided.

They eat a lot of fish products and drink a lot of green tea. Green tea has a ph of around 9.0. I worked with Okinawan civilians there that were older gents. I’m not sure how old they were, but they had excellent mobility and didn’t seem to ever be in much pain.

When I am out in public, I have my water with me in dark blue bottles to preserve the freshness of the charge from my machine. This attracts people who have questions about where they can buy the water. We have regressed since the 1960’s to the common practice of buying water. I no longer drink anything that does not come from my machine other than some milk. I make my coffee out of it.

I have never experienced anything that makes me feel like the water from the machine does. I found out that my diet wasn’t just about what I eat, it is also about what I drink. If you do not currently have one of these machines in your home, then the water you are drinking is a joke. Unfortunately, it isn’t funny like the joke listed above once was.
