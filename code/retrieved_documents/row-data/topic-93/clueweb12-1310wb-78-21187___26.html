Our take on the Sonos® Play:3 (S3)

by Tara W.

All the music you want — anywhere in your home

Access PANDORA® Internet radio, Rhapsody®, Spotify, iHeartRadio, Last.fm, Napster®, SiriusXM, and 100,000 free Internet radio stations — no matter where you are in your home. The compact Sonos Play:3 lets you enjoy music from the Internet as well as songs stored on your home computers — without filling your room with audio gear. It's perfect for small rooms and cozy corners, and setup is as easy as pushing a button.

Expand to any (or every) room

Place Sonos players, like the Play:3 and the larger Play:5, in every room where you want to listen to music. The players connect to the Internet and communicate with each other using Sonos' proprietary wireless network. If you have your router in a room where you won't have a Sonos player, use aBridge to connect to your network.

You probably already own a Sonos controller

You can use an iPhone®, iPod touch®, iPad®, or Android™ phone to control any and all Sonos players. A free download turns your smartphone and tablet into a full-fledged Sonos controller. Easily navigate and search through playlists and tracks stored on your computer, or select an Internet radio station. Create customized playlists on the fly for individual rooms or for playback over your entire Sonos system. You can also mute or adjust the volume of each of your players.

Rhapsody®, PANDORA®, SIRIUS, and more at your fingertips

The Sonos system works seamlessly with a host of Internet-based digital music services, including Rhapsody, PANDORA, last.fm™, Napster®, and SIRIUS Internet Radio. Rhapsody offers instant access to millions of songs and ad-free radio stations. SIRIUS Internet Radio gives you access to over 80 channels of programming. Every Sonos system includes free 30-day trials of these two services, so you can start listening the moment you take it out of the box. PANDORA and last.fm create customized Internet radio streams just for you — all you have to do is enter the name of your favorite song or artist. And because Sonos connects directly to the Internet for all of these music services, you don't even have to turn on your PC.

MOG — millions of songs, available instantly

MOG is an up-and-coming online music service with one of the largest and best-sounding streaming catalogs in the industry. It's the only service to offer all of its music in the 320 kbps MP3 format — that higher bit rate translates to better sound. In fact, we use it frequently in the Crutchfield Labs to test high-end audio gear. MOG's "Primo" service ($9.99/month) gives you unlimited, ad-free access to their extensive music library on a wide range of devices including Sonos® network music players, Samsung TVs, your computer, iPad®, iPhone®, and Android™ devices.

Expand your Sonos system

You can have up to 32 ZonePlayers in your Sonos system, and each one can play an independent selection of individual tracks or playlists. Listen to jazz in the den while your spouse plays rock music in the kitchen, or pump a single playlist of songs throughout the entire house during a party.

Overview: The Sonos Play:3 is a wireless HiFi speaker system that lets you stream your entire music library, music services, and Internet radio stations from your wireless home network.  The Sonos Play:3 lets you use your iPod touch, iPhone, iPad, or any Sonos Controller (616CR200, sold separately) to control your music. The Play:3 creates room-filling, high-performance sound using an integrated three-way speaker system driven by three built-in digital amplifiers. In a multi-room sound system, the first Play:3 is connected to your home network via Ethernet or using the SONOS Bridge (616BR100, sold separately), and each subsequent Play:3 can be wired or completely wireless.

Note: To get started, you must wire either one Sonos Play:3 or Sonos Bridge (sold separately) to your home network using a standard Ethernet cable (included). The Sonos Play:3 works seamlessly with the complete range of Sonos products.

Wireless Technology

SonosNet 2.0 Wireless Mesh Network: The Sonos Play:3 brings great sounding music wirelessly to any room in your house, without a big wiring project. SonosNet 2.0, a proprietary wireless mesh network technology, provides superior wireless range for whole-house coverage, ensures synchronous music playback, and avoids sources of wireless interference. The transmitting range of the Play:3's built-in wireless system is approximately 100 feet, depending upon your home's layout.

Simple Wireless Setup: No technical or wireless expertise is required to install the Sonos Play:3. Simply, place a Play:3 in any room where you want music, plug it in and enjoy. To get started, you must wire one PLAY:3 or a Sonos Bridge (616BR100, sold separately) to your router to access your home's network; via standard Ethernet cable (included). Then the Sonos System Setup software provides simple on-screen prompts on any Sonos controller, computer, iPod touch, iPhone, iPad, or Android. With the touch of a few buttons, you'll be playing music all over the house in minutes.

Multi-Zone Compatibility: You can place a Play:3 in each zone where you want to listen to music. The Sonos Digital Music System can accept up to 32 players. With multiple zones, you can play the same song in different rooms, or different songs in different rooms.

2-Way Ethernet Port: When wirelessly connected to a Sonos Bridge or another Sonos component, you can use the Ethernet port on the back of the Play:3 to bring standard Internet connectivity to your Satellite/Cable set-top box, DVR, PC, game console, or network hard drive.

Audio Section

3 Driver/3 Amp Speaker System: The Sonos Play:3 uses three state-of the-art digital amplifiers individually coupled with three speakers to deliver great sound to any room. The wireless music system provides pure, clean sound that is perfect for bringing room filling music to a kitchen, bedroom, office, or just about any room in your home. A bass radiator produces powerful low notes while, two mid-range drivers and one tweeter fills out the sound.

  • Class-D Amp: The Play:3 employs state of the art, great sounding amplifiers that are lightweight, powerful, and very energy efficient in standby and use.
  • Tweeter: One tweeter creates a crisp and accurate high frequency response.
  • 2 Midranges: Two dedicated mid-range drivers ensure the faithful reproduction of midrange frequencies crucial for accurate playback of vocals and instruments.
  • Bass Radiator: A bass radiator is tuned to use the energized air volume inside of Play:3 to provide deep, clean and surprisingly powerful bass from such a small package

DSP Circuitry: From the recording studio to your home, the music is reproduced as the artist intended. All filter settings, active equalization, and time-alignment are done in the digital domain through state-of-the-art DSP circuitry so there's absolutely no loss of audio quality or energy.

Tone Controls: Optimize sound performance using zone-specific bass, treble, balance, and loudness controls. Adjust volume by individual zone or by groups of zones.

Stereo Pairing: The Stereo Pair setting allows you to group two Play:3s in the same room to create a wider stereo experience. In this configuration, one unit serves as the left channel and the other serves as the right channel.

Note: When creating a stereo pair, it is best to place the two Sonos components 8' to 10' away from each other, angled inward to form a triangle with your favorite listening position.

Music Streaming

Music Streaming: The Sonos Play:3 lets you play your entire music library, listen to your online music services, and get thousands of free radio stations worldwide. 

Internet Radio (100,000 free stations included): Tune in to 100,000 Internet radio stations, shows & podcasts; free of charge and pre-loaded on the Sonos Play:3. You can browse endlessly or type in the call letters or name of your favorite station or host and hit Search. Tune in to live radio shows on everything from politics to sports to entertainment, catch up on what you missed with podcasts featuring the best of previous shows, or see what is playing soon. You can listen internationally or locally; just tell it where you live and it will give you all the local radio stations streaming on the Internet. You can even add to it to Favorites so it's easy to find the next time. The unit supports MP3 and WMA Internet streaming.

Online Music Services: A music service is an online music store or online service that sells audio on a per song, per audiobook, or subscription basis. Sonos works seamlessly with Anubis.fm, AUPEO!, Deezer, iheartradio, iTunes, Last.fm, MOG, Napster, Pandora, Rdio, Rhapsody, SiriusXM, Spotify, Stitcher SmartRadio, TuneIn, Wolfgang's Vault, and downloads from any service offering DRM-free tracks.

Music Service Description
AUPEO! AUPEO! Personal Radio is a music streaming service based in Berlin that is available worldwide. With AUPEO! Personal Radio, you can listen to free music on the web. The idea is very simple - AUPEO! play lots of music and let the listener choose the playlist based on their personal taste. The basic concept is based on personal music DNA, which allows you to 'love' or 'ban' songs. The highly advanced software then remembers this and adjusts as necessary. As a result, we offer over 100 compilation genre channels, as well as thousands of artist channels. Listen to music online based on your tastes and preferences or use our one-of-a-kind 'Mood Tuner' to fit how you're feeling.
Deezer Deezer is a music streaming service based in Paris, France. Deezer offers access to 6,5 million tracks. You can then create and share your playlists.
iheartradio iheartradio delivers access to over 750 Clear Channel local radio stations in both AM and FM, as well as commercial-free and artist-programmed channels. All stations are streamed in high-quality AAC format. iheart radio provides  song & artist information, as well as Album Art. You can select available stations from Favorites, Featured, Local Stations, All Cities, National Stations, Genres, and Personalities. iheartradio can also randomly select stations for you.
iTunes Play your entire iTunes library stored on any computer. Play your entire DRM-free iTunes library, not just what fits on your iPod. Instantly access any iTunes library stored on any computer or Network Attached Storage.
Last.FM  Simply enter the name of your favorite artist or genre and Last.fm will create personal radio stations based on your musical tastes. You can also listen by genre with Tag Radio, discover new music based on your past listening with Recommendations Radio or explore what other Last.fm users with similar tastes are listening to with Neighborhood Radio. At any time, simply touch the Info View button to find out more about the music that's playing, like detailed artist bios, tags, and upcoming events. Turn on "scrobbling" and get the most relevant music recommendations based on your listening habits.
MOG MOG on-demand listening service provides access to a deep library of over 11 million songs and a million albums.
Napster 8 million songs at your fingertips. That's the beauty of Napster and Sonos. Just type in the name of your favorite artist or track and start listening to a recent release or a classic from high school; it's all there in Napster's massive music library. Discover new music and rediscover old favorites with top 100s, Napster playlists, radio, charts, and more. Or create a custom playlist based on any artist or song with Napster Automix.
Pandora Create custom stations based on your favorite artists and songs with Pandora. It's a new kind of radio; personalized just for you, playing only the music you love. Rate songs as they play by choosing thumbs up or down on your Sonos Controller and Pandora will adapt the station on the fly based upon your feedback. Bookmark songs as they play in case you want to download them later. Works seamlessly with your Pandora iPhone app so you can create stations when you are out on the town and then come home and listen on the couch.
Rdio Rdio give you access to over 8 million songs. You are able to search and find any song, album or artist, and start listening immediately. You can search music by new releases and chart toppers. You can also check out what other listeners have been listening to the most, or look at your own recent listening history. In addition, you can even create your own music collection or make your own playlists. Rdio will even recommend music for you, based on your previous music listening. Rdio also provides Artist, Album, and Song information; as well as Album art.
Rhapsody Enjoy instant access to Rhapsody's catalog of millions of songs and ad-free music channels, right from the palm of your hand. Browse by genre or check out the charts. Find what you love by typing in the name of your favorite artist, album, composer or track. Get new album and playlist recommendations based upon the music you have chosen. And build your own library of favorites and create custom playlists so you can listen again and again. To stay in the know about the music that is playing, simply touch the Info View button and get artist bios, photos, projects, album reviews, and more.
SiriusXM SiriusXM Internet Radio lets you listen to over 120 channels of commercial-free music, talk radio, news, sports, and more.
Spotify The award-winning music service that's taken Europe by storm has now come to United States. Millions of tracks ready to play instantly. Scour through a library of 13 million songs. Create custom playlists, and share with your friends.
Stitcher SmartRadio Create your own personalized list of podcasts and tune in to the most up-to-date content in business, sports, politics, and entertainment from brands like NPR, CNN, BBC, Adam Carolla and more. Pull together your favorites from over 5000 podcasts, radio shows and stations to create a custom playlist, or allow Stitcher SmartRadio to recommend shows based on past listening.
Tune-In Radio TuneIn is the global leader in delivering free local, international and Internet radio. TuneIn helps listeners quickly and easily find their favorite radio stations and personalities plus discover new ones. With a directory of over 50,000 AM/FM and Internet only radio stations listeners can experience the whole world of radio within a single application at home.
Wolfgang's Vault Listen like you were there, with performances from the world's largest collection of live concerts. Search over 3,500 concerts featuring over 40,000 songs from the last 50 years - from Miles Davis to The Who. Browse by artist, genre, specific concert, or search Bill Graham's personal archives. Add concerts and tracks to your personal playlist to play anywhere in your home.

Note: Service availability varies by region.

Music Libraries: The Sonos Play:3 wireless music system can play music from any computer or network-attached storage (NAS) device on your home network where you have shared music folders. The Sonos Play:3 can play music stored on up to 16 PCs, Macs or NAS (Network Attached Storage) devices on your home network, supporting the most popular audio formats. The Sonos Play:3 indexes your music folders so you can view your music collection by categories (such as artists, albums, composers, genres, or tracks). The Sonos Play:3 supports the following audio file formats - MP3 (compressed), WMA (compressed), AAC (MPEG4), iTunes, Ogg Vorbis, Audible (format 4), Apple Lossless, Flac (lossless), WAV (uncompressed), and AIFF (uncompressed).

Note: Previously purchased Apple "Fairplay" DRM-protected songs may need to be upgraded. WMA Lossless format not currently supported.

Optional iPod Dock: Dock, charge and wirelessly play the music from an iPod or iPhone with the Sonos DOCK (616WD100, sold separately). The Sonos Wireless Dock 100 lets you play music from select iPods or iPhones on a Sonos Wireless Multi-Room Music System throughout your home. You can wirelessly play all the music that's stored or playing (including many music apps) on your iPod or iPhone in all the rooms of  your home or office where you have Sonos.

Controllers

System Control: The Sonos Zone Play:3 provides control of your music using a Sonos Controller (sold separately), iPod touch, iPhone, iPad, Android Smartphone, or desktop controller software.

Sonos Controller: The Sonos Zone Controller (616CR200, sold separately) works exclusively with the Sonos Digital Music Wireless Systems, and allow you to control and view all of your music and all of your zones wirelessly from the palm of your hand. You can even view Album Art. The controller communicates with the nearest Sonos Player through Sonosnet, a secure, peer-to-peer wireless mesh network, allowing you to extend the range of the controller to any room in your house, even outside. Because it communicates with the nearest Sonos Player, rather than through one distant control point, the system avoids sources of wireless interference. The range of the Sonosnet is 50 to 100 feet, a range similar to that of an 802.11g wireless access point.

Controller Apps: The Sonos Play:3 can be controlled from your iPod touch, iPhone, iPad, or Android smartphone. Free Sonos Controller apps for Android, iPhone, iPod touch, and iPad let you control your zone players, music on the Internet and your computer right from the palm of your hand. You are able to search for songs and stations, choose the music, and control the volume. You can also view song and artist information, as well as Album Art. You can even get a text or a call on your iPhone or Android smartphone and the music will keep playing.

Note: Download the apps for free on the iTunes App Store or Android Market. Your iPod touch, iPhone, or iPad must have iOS 3.0 or later installed. Your smartphone must have Android 2.1 or up installed.

Top-Panel Controls: The Play:3 Provides quick access to volume and mute controls when you don't have a remote controller in hand.

Desktop Controller Software: The supplied setup disc contains the software for the Desktop Controller, which allows you to control the Sonos Digital Music System from your home computer. It includes easy-to-use wizards for setup and music sharing so the music folders on your hard drive can be shared across all your Sonos players. You can also add Internet Radio stations and set preferences for online updates. Once the system is set up, all you need to do is choose your music, choose your zone, and press Play. The Desktop Controller is compatible with Windows XP and higher, as well as Macintosh OS X (10.4 and higher) operating systems.

Mounting Options

Versatile Placement: You can place the Sonos Play:3 wherever you find the sound most enjoyable. Its compact size makes it easy to place the Play:3 on your desk, the kitchen counter, on your nightstand, or carry it out to the patio with you.

Note: The Sonos Play:3 is not water-resistant; do not leave it outside during a rain storm or position it near water. Care should be taken if placing the Sonos Play:3 close to an older CRT television. If you notice any discoloration or distortion of your picture quality, simply move the Play:3 further from the television.

Horizontal/Vertical Mounting: Position the Play:3 in a horizontal or vertical orientation to make a perfect fit into tight spots around you home like on bookshelves, nightstands, corners, or just about anywhere else. The EQ automatically adjusts so the sound you hear is always great, not matter what orientation you use.

Note: For optimum performance, maintaining more than 1" clearance around the back of the unit is recommended.

Rubber Feet: Integrated rubber feet protect your furniture and keep the Play:3 secure.

Wall-Mount (optional): The Sonos Play:3 has a 1/4"-20 integrated, threaded mounting hole. If desired, you can easily wall mount the Play:3 using a suitable mounting bracket (sold separately). Simply choose a location that's close to an electrical outlet, taking care to avoid heat, steam, dripping water or hidden wiring, and follow the instructions included with your wall mount kit.

Note: Be sure the wall mount bracket you purchase will support at least 10 lbs.

Network Requirements

Hardware: In order to play the digital music files stored on your computer throughout the Sonos Digital Music System, you must meet the following hardware requirements:

  • Ethernet: You must have an Ethernet network interface connection in your computer. If you do not have one, you will need to buy and install a Network Interface Card (NIC) before setting up your system.
  • Modem: It is highly recommended that you have a DSL/cable modem, or LAN-based high-speed Internet connection. It is not designed for a dial-up system.
  • Router: If you have a cable or DSL modem connected directly to your computer, you should have a router connected between your modem and your computer to maintain your computer's security. If you don't have a router, you will need to buy and install one before setting up your system.

System Requirements: In addition to the above-described hardware, your computer must have the following:

  • Compatible Operating Systems: Windows XP (SP2) and higher, Macintosh OS X (10.4 and higher)
  • CPU: 500 MHz processor or better
  • RAM: 128MB RAM minimum/256MB RAM recommended

Automatic Software Updates: Once the Sonos Play:3 system has been registered, Sonos will automatically notify you when software updates are available. Simply press one button and your system updates itself.

Sonos Play3

Crutchfield Customer Review (What's this?)
Crutchfield Customer Review

The reviewer purchased this item from us, verified by the email address on the order.


  I love this little player--its good qualities far outweigh its cons. However, consider well what you want to use it for before you purchase (Sonos and Crutchfield customer service are both excellent). The volume and sound quality are good enough to enjoy the full range of sound when playing music and to surmount the noise of housework, paper shuffling, etc. Setup was easy (but be sure you insert the sonos end of the A/C fully--until the "shoulder" of the cord is flush or below the casing or you will have intermittent function!). Network range is not quite as good as with my computers but still excellent. Pros:
Great sound, portable. Great for radio (especially those of us who have no reception over the air). Easily synced to most iTunes content. Easy setup.
Cons:
Biggest cons: Terribly expensive controller if you don't have smart phone, etc.; no input port. Other: no easy way to stream podcasts, etc., directly from browser or browser download, no way I've found to stream content stored on iPhone, etc., to Play3 without a networked, awake computer (If I've missed something here, I'd love some advice). Audiobooks must 1st be imported into iTunes (9.0.2, Mac OS Tiger 10.4.11)--syncing & playback of this content via Sonos app is not intuitive.
