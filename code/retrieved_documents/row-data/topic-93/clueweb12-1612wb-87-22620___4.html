A selection of useful water words and definitions are listed below in alphabetical order. You can use them to learn new words as you read the educational stories on this web site. And of course you canContact Us if you have questions or want to learn more!

Acid Rain: Rain water that has a lower pH (acidic) as a result of sulfur gases being released into the atmosphere from the burning of coal, gasoline and other fossil fuels.

Aquifer: An underground rock formation that can hold water.

Chlorination: The process by which chlorine is added to water during treatment in order to kill harmful microorganisms.

Chlorine: One of the most common chemicals used in the treatment of public water supplies because it is highly effective in killing harmful microorganisms.

Condensation: The process by which water vapor collects into clouds of tiny water droplets in the cooler atmosphere.

Conservation: Methods of using water wisely with the minimum amount of water being wasted.

Contaminant: Any substance found in water that may be harmful to water consumers.

Contamination: The introduction of a harmful substance into the water either at the supply or during distribution. Contamination can occur naturally or by the hands of humans.

Drought: A period of lower-than-normal precipitation that can lead to a water shortage.

Evaporation: The process by which water from the earth's surface is transported into the atmosphere as it turns from liquid to gas.

Filtration: The process by which organic and inorganic particles are removed from the raw water.

Groundwater: Any source of water that is found beneath the earth's surface in an aquifer.

Hardness: The texture of water that contains high levels of harmless metals such as magnesium and calcium. The term called "hard water" is used because it's "hard" to get lather from soap and shampoo when using this water.

Hydrologic Cycle: Also called the Water Cycle. This is the natural cycle of water, including evaporation, transpiration, condensation, precipitation and percolation.

Intake: The part of a surface water treatment facility where water from a lake, river or stream is drawn into the plant for filtration, treatment and distribution to customers.

Lead: A metal that is harmful to infants and children if swallowed. An older home containing lead pipes could contribute to increased lead levels in the home's tap water.

Back to top

Percolation: The process by which precipitation replenishes groundwater sources. After a period of rain, snow, sleet or hail, water will flow through soil and rock to reach underground aquifers.

Potable Water: Water that is safe to drink after the raw water has gone through the filtration and treatment process.

Precipitation: Any form of water falling from clouds down to earth, which replenishes water supplies. This includes rain, snow, hail, sleet and freezing rain.

Private Well: A well that is intended to provide water to a single home or building.

Public Water Supply: Water provided to a group of homes or an entire city through a water utility via underground pipes and water mains.

Raw Water: The water from a surface water or groundwater source prior to filtration or treatment.

Recharge Area: An area of land that allows rainwater to percolate into the earth's surface to replenish groundwater sources.

Recharge: The increase in groundwater levels after rainwater soaks into the ground and into an aquifer.

Back to top

Reservoir: A man-made storage area for water. Sometimes a reservoir will be open, like a man-made lake, or it may be a fully enclosed tank, either above-ground or underground.

Service Lateral: A pipe that runs underground from a home or building to a water utility's water main in order to provide that home or building with public water.

Surface Water: Any source of water that is found on top of the earth's surface, such as a lake, river or stream.

Transpiration: The process by which plants produce water vapor which is released into the atmosphere.

Treatment: The process by which substances are added to the water in order to make it safe to drink or look and taste better.

Water Main: The underground pipe used to transport potable water from a water utility to your home.

Water Meter: A device that records the amount of water being used in your home. Your water utility checks your water meter regularly because your water bill is based on how much water you use.

Water Pollution: Usually referring to the introduction of a harmful substance into the water supply as a result of human actions.

Water Softener: A device used within the home to reduce the hardness of the water.

Watershed: Also called a Drainage Basin. This is an area of land that allows rainwater to flow into creeks, streams and rivers.

Well: A deep hole in the ground that is dug in order to gain access to groundwater sources.
