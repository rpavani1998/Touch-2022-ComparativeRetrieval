    Thanks for saying this...while there are a few suspicious liftings on Family Guy's part...people are really going out of their way to find any and all similarities they can and labelling them "plagiarism".

    You can do the same by taking the Flintstones vs The Simpsons. The reality is that nothing comes from a creative vacuum--writers, all good ones, are moreso collectors and imaginative thieves than anything close to the metaphysical wellsprings of ideas people like to think they are. They steal, from their real lives, from other people's work, and jigsaw them together to come up with something that is, in the end, unique...and then the process repeats.

    Plagiarism is copying without bothering to make it sufficiently unique from what you're assembling your work from.

    Not saying Family Guy (or American Dad....which is a....less defesnible story) is _completely_ innocent of this, but there are really only like 2 or 3 infractions that hold any water in the face of common sense.

    I mean...seriously watch almost any given Spoony video and keep a tally of how many MST3K riffs he uses. It's not plagiarism, of course, but....
