Audi A4 vs BMW 3 Series

Carlton Boyce

/

March 23, 2012

The middleweight executive car market is one of the most fiercely contested there is, with every premium manufacturer looking for the biggest possible slice of the pie.    Quality, image, reliability, purchase price, residual value, performance and fuel efficiency are the cards that everyone is shuffling, and no-one deals them better than Audi with their A4 and BMW with their 3 Series.  
  Audi are newer to the market but no less familiar, mainly because they sell more cars in the UK than BMW. This is a good thing – and a bad one. It produces turnover and profit but diminishes exclusivity and with exclusivity comes image.   Who does it best then? Who makes the best all-rounder executive car? There’s only one way to find out…  

Looks

The new BMW is a variation on the familiar 3 Series design that we’ve all come to know, love, and lust after. The 2012 car is a new model, yet retains the corporate DNA - you’ll never mistake it for any other marque. Yet it looks slightly fussy to our eyes; it seems to be trying just that little bit too hard and there is just a bit too much gilding around the front end for it to look truly wonderful.  
  Which isn’t an accusation that can be levelled at the Audi A4, which after its recent facelift is a simpler design, more effortless, and sober. It’s as if the original designer had his own way entirely with no input from the marketing department or the grey suits in accounts; he drew, they produced.    The LED front lights scowl, and the bonnet and front wings slide into the A-post like water onto glass. We think it is easily the most handsome car in the segment, which is an astonishing accomplishment given the main players involved.  
  There are no losers here though, as both cars look great; they ooze class and elegance and no-one is going to mistake the driver of either for someone who gets his hands dirty at work.   

Interior

The interior of both cars is well designed, impeccably specified, and beautifully installed. Both will soothe your workday stresses and both are big enough to accommodate your family and impress three of your colleagues.  
  The Audi is slightly more restrained and sober, which we think is a good thing. The BMW looks a bit more fiddly and yet simultaneously more expensive. Both are stuffed with neat pieces of design that wouldn’t look out of place on a high-end stereo.  
  Really, the best car will depend on your own personal taste, but most of the journalists that have driven both cars tend to favour the BMW, giving it its first win.  

Standard equipment

Both cars have a decent level of equipment as standard – and both can quickly become very expensive when you start to dig deep into the options list!    The A4, in 2.0 TDI 143 SE spec, gets climate control, cruise control, rear parking sensors, cloth interior and 17" wheels as standard. It's priced at £29,110.
  The broadly comparable BMW 318d SE also gets climate control, cruise control rear parking sensors, cloth interior and 17" wheels. It's priced at £26,925.   They are fairly similar in terms of standard kit, but be careful about how much the options that are important to you cost on each car.  
 

Driving

This is the weakest category for the A4, as it just can’t match the 3 Series’ chassis. The Audi suffers from the customarily firm ride (especially when it is fitted with large wheels) and poor steering feel that is rapidly becoming an Audi trademark.    The BMW has fluidity and a responsiveness that the Audi simply can’t match and so it takes a well-deserved win here. The simple truth is that few people make cars that drive as well as BMW.  

Engines

The most popular engines in both cars are the high-end diesels, bought because they give great fuel efficiency and an effortless wave of torque that makes for relaxed driving; important when you are pounding the roads of the UK day after day.   BMW’s ubiquitous 320d engine offers 181bhp and a potential fuel economy of 62.8mpg. This is no economy-optimised wimp of an engine though; it delivers sparkling performance (0-60 in 7.5 secs) and, whilst it might not be the most refined engine in its class, it is still impressively quiet.   
  The Audi A4 puts up a compelling argument of its own too: 177bhp from the 2.0-litre turbodiesel engine is impressive, but the experts say that it is the quality of delivery that is so impressive, They love its free-revving nature and smooth, torquey power output. It’ll top 140mph, gets to 60 in 8.2  and returns 61.4mpg;  impressive figures and likely to be enough for most drivers.   Nonetheless, the BMW just takes the edge in this category, although it is a narrow victory.  

Price

The BMW 3 Series range starts at £22,695 for the 318i ES and rises all the way to £38,950 for the 335d M Sport. Our benchmark 320 in SE guise costs £27,900 and comes with Stop/Start, air-con and the sort of toys that are almost obligatory at this level.    The Audi A4 range matches that of the BMW pretty closely as you’d imagine, stretching from £23,625 for the 1.8 TFSI to the 3.0 TDI quattro at £38,405.   The 2.0-litre TDI SE with 177bhp costs £27,575 in SE form, which just undercuts theBMW 320d SE which costs £28,025.
  The Benefit In Kind (BIK) figures for both cars are broadly similar too. Residual values are on a par, just take a look atmotors.co.uk for proof.
 
 

Expert Reviews

Here at carbuzz we compile and summarise expert reviews for each car. The BMW reviews have been unanimously positive, everyone who has driven it absolutely loves it.   That’s not to say the A4 is a bad car, far from it. The reviews of the 2012 A4 have been largely positive, praising the revisions made and new line-up of engines. However from reading the BMW reviews, it's clear that the experts say it's clearly the better car.   

Verdict

There’s no doubting really that the general consensus is that the BMW is the better car. It's more modern and a quick look at the 3-Series review page shows it's an outstanding car.    We’d suggest that you drive both and make up your mind for yourself because they might look similar on paper but they’re very different to drive – and own; Brian Ferry would choose an A4 because it is so effortlessly cool and offers a Bauhaus sobriety that the BMW just doesn’t. It’s an intellectual car, not a sporting one.   Whereas Steve McQueen would choose the 3 Series; it’s sublime chassis and handling keep it at the top of the list for those customers for whom driving is all about how a car feels round the bends. If you’ve ever wanted to go to the Nürburgring then this is the car for you.   You wouldn’t be unhappy to have either car on your drive, but the one that you choose probably says more about you than you’d care to admit…   Check out our full guide to each of the cars, with reviews, user reviews, videos, photos and stats:    Audi A4 guide
