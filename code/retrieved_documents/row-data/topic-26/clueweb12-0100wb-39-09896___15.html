2012 Detroit: Lincoln MKZ Concept is Latest Brand-Changing Car

Written by: Christian Seabaugh G+ on January 10 2012 6:00 AM

 




Ford Group VP Jim Farley and Lincoln design chief Max Wolff pulled the wraps off the new Lincoln MKZ Concept today in Cobo Hall. The MKZ Concept previews Lincoln’s new design direction as it looks to snag young buyers away from the “efficient but soulless” German luxury manufacturers.

The Lincoln MKZ Concept is a real big deal for Lincoln. As its best seller, Lincoln can’t afford to make the MKZ ‘just’ a rebadged Ford Fusion. If the MKZ Concept is any indication it looks as if Lincoln is finally readying to step out on its own.

While it still shares its chassis and architecture with the Fusion, the MKZ Concept takes a decidedly different approach. Where the Fusion looks like an Aston Martin midsize sedan with a loud front end, large haunches and flared wheel arches, the MKZ Concept goes for a more subdued, refined look.

Up front, the MKZ Concept takes Lincoln’s current waterfall grille and turns it 90 degrees, letting it flow into the new LED headlights. Lincoln takes a page out of Audi andMercedes-Benz’s playbook for the MKZ Concept’s profile, which is almost coupe-like with a long sweeping roofline. Speaking of which, Lincoln went for a full-length panoramic glass roof for the MKZ Concept that visually flows from the car’s hood line to the trunk lid.

The MKZ Concept’s interior, like the exterior, is elegant and contemporary; it gets attractive looking aluminum and recycled Poplar trim, and is bathed in champagne-colored leather that contrasts nicely with the brown dashboard. The interior also gets MyLincoln Touch, and a large LCD screen that functions as the MKZ’s instrument panel. The neatest interior touch though would have to be the MKZ Concept’s push-button transmission. Sure it’s gimmicky, but if Jaguar can have a rotary shifter, why can’t Lincoln do push-button?

Engine availability is currently unknown but rumor has it the next Lincoln MKZ may get a 2.7-liter EcoBoost V-6 that won’t be seen in any other Ford products, in addition to the 1.6 and 2.0-liter Ecoboosts, and the 2.0-liter hybrid version.
