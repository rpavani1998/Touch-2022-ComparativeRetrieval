Thread of the Day: Four-Door “Coupes.” Are They Your Cup of Tea?

Written by: Benson Kong on May 13 2010 5:00 PM

 




Look the word “coupe” up in the dictionary and the majority of the definitions suggest two-door cars. Combine the words “four-door” with “coupe” and you incite controversy, as Mercedes-Benz quickly found out with its CLS-Class. Today’s Thread of the Day wants to know what you think of four-door “coupes.”



Are they sleek and sexy? Or are they a waste of automaker funding? Either way, let the remarks commence in the comments below.

Thanks to Pandabear for inspiring today’s TOTD!

  13. Bearcat Posted on: May 14, 2010 4:31 am Reply

    The term “two door sedan” has been around ever since the 50′s and is perfectly legitimate. It just meant a 2-door car with a pillar between the side windows, as opposed to a 2-door hardtop, which didn’t have one. And any 4-door sedan usually had its equivalent 4-door hardtop model. So in a hardtop, if the windows were down, you could run your hand from the wing window to the C-pillar without hitting anything. The sedans, especially the 2-door ones, were considered rather low in prestige; the hardtops cost more and were seen as much cooler, tho yes, the windows would soon rattle or leak and hardtops were structurally awful in a roll-over.
