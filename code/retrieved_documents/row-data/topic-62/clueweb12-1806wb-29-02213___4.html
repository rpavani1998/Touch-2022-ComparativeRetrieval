Posted June 26, 2011 12:48 PM Hide Post

Your orchid is a Phaelanopsis(sp?) - aka "Moth Orchid". They're lovely easy plants - a perfect beginner's orchid. I have had several for many years now, & even when I've been neglectful they still continue to perform for me. Bright indirect light is best - mine are on an east-facing windowsill above the kitchen sink. As stated on other posts - well-draining orchid medium is best, which usually consists mostly of shredded bark. And as also stated, terracotta orchid pots with the little slits in the sides are best for good drainage & aeration. Although I don't know how "organic" it is, I also use a fertilizer specifically meant for orchids. Most garden centers &/or box stores carry it. Used as directed, & clipping off the bloom stems once they wither, can have your orchid blooming almost continuously.

The only pest I've ever had a problem with is Mealybug, which seems to arrive on every orchid I've purchased or received. A few applications of Safer insecticidal soap keeps things under control.

As for the Peace Lily - bright indirect light works best. They burn easily in hot bright situations. Soil kept just moist works best; overwatering will yellow & rot them.

I despise those pots that orchids are sold in! The best thing I've found so far for the pitiful ones rescued off the sales rack are decorative pots with lots of holes. Those made for potpouri or votive candles work great for small orchids (I managed to find several dirt cheap at a thrift shop).

Like BG, a north window and moderately moist soil works for peace lilies and orchids--I have one large and 2 small phals that are plump-leaved and producing new roots and leaves, plus a variegated peace lily putting out new leaves.
