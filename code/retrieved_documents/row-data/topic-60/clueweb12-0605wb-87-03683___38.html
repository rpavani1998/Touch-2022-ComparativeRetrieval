posted September 09, 2004 08:55 PM                      Since people can't seem to keep this issue out of the football thread, we should discuss it here.

I, for one, prefer football over soccer for some of the following reasons:

Football has more strategy. Each play is a battle of wits between the offense and the defense. Each side fights for the advantage, trying to dictate the play to the other team. You have to anticipate what your enemy is going to bring, and choose your own strategy accordingly, all before the ball is snapped. Every play is a mini chess game, and requires much higher levels of thinking than soccer. What startegies are there in soccer? Shrink the defense to strengthen the offense or vice versa? Choose between long passes, short passes, or dribbling? Is that it? It's so simple a child could figure it out (which is probably why so many American kids play it [I know I did] before they grow up and start to enjoy the more sophisticated game of football).

Another problem I have with soccer is the players. Any game that encourages someone to fake an injury pisses me off. Watching these prima donnas flop around on the field because someone stepped on their toe makes me sick. And what makes it worse is that everyone knows that they're faking: the crowd, the other players, the coaches, the commentators, even the refs! And they still get away with it! When was the last time you remember an NFL player trying to pull that? And how could anyone compare the dangers of a soccer match with that of a football game? These people slam into each other at full speed play after play, get up and ask "you want some more, b*tch?" There's no comparison. I was watching the Stanley Cup playoffs last year, and some of the players started to pull that crap in order to get power plays. I threw up and then cursed the soccer gods for their unholy influence on a once noble sport!

Another problem I have with soccer is the pace. The game isso slow, and I'm saying this as someone who is promoting a game with plays that last approximately 4 seconds. The play is concentrated in the middle of the field, where the players give the ball back and forth to each other until someone breaks down the field and shoots the ball 20 feet wide of the net. Then the goalie kicks the ball back to the middle of the field and we start all over again. 2 goals are considered a nearly insurmountable lead, and a team with the lead is content to sit back and kill 50 minutes of the game. In football, on the other hand, each play is executed at full speed, with full effort. If you don't want to give it your all, get off the field. And no lead is safe (ask Oilers' fans). You don't give up until the QB takes a knee and says checkmate.

Finally, I believe football is better, because it's the ultimate fan game. Fans can track every minute detail of their teams, second-guess every decision, stress about every injury, argue endlessly about a third and 5, and even build their own teams. How many fantasy soccer leagues are out there? How many video game soccer tournaments are held? Where's the hubbub over Beckham 2005?

Football is a strategic war between two teams of highly specialized players. Soccer is hockey's retarded little brother. Posts: 622 | Registered: Jul 2004  |  IP: Logged |

posted September 10, 2004 04:01 PM                      Hannibal, you say that a 20-10 football game is the equivalent of a 2-1 soccer game, because they count each touchdown as 7 points. But the scoring adds a whole new dimension to the game. It adds risk. Do you go for the win by scoring a more difficult TD, or do you take the sure points with a FG? And a touchdown isn't even worth 7 points. It's worth 6. Then you have to decide whether to take the sure PAT or risk everything to go for 2. Do you try to win/tie in one possession, or trust your defense to get the ball back so you can win in two?

In soccer, your options are, "well, I think we'll try to score 1 point, and if that fails, we'll try to score 1 point, but we can always go for 1 point." So both teams are trying to do the same thing regardless. It's just not as interesting.

And what do you mean, "what strategy is there in football?"? Every team has a playbook that weighs 10 pounds!
