1.) A book on youth basketball coaching philosophy with a focus on basketball fundamentals, fun and creating memorable experiences,

 

2.) A basketball practice plan guide (includes thirty, 90-minute practice plans) so you’ll be ready for every sessions — includes pre-practice meeting agendas,

 

3.) A series of basketball forms (including a “shot tracker” and “basketball homework” form), and

 

4.) A basketball award certificate maker (customize the forms to your team and players’ names).

He recounts how he overcomes his OCCD — “Overly Competitive Coaching Disorder” — to become a better coach, mentor and parent.

If, as a youth baseball coach, you would rather have fun, structured, fast‐moving practices loaded with fundamentals, then this book is for you. As you enter a new baseball season, three things are certain; your players will be diverse in their skills and understanding of the game, there is great deal your players will need to learn, and finally, you will have a limited amount of time to prepare for each practice, as well as a limited amount of time during practice.
