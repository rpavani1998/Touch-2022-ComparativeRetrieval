BC Cleanup

Feb 1

Written by: c2itconsult
2/1/2011 10:15 AM


     The weather was a little rainy in the middle of the week, cooler towards the weekend.  Lots of campers in the campground and lots of campfires were used to keep everyone warm and toasty.                                    

     The Chain Saw Crew, led by Greg Hersberger, had several members including Bob and Debbie McPheeters, Mike Slusser, Harry Rice, Lee and Ethan Hindsley, Scott Chaney, Don Eutley, Jim and Maggie Pitser, Bob Montgomery, Norma Heistand, Rob and Nancy Brunnemer and Chris Lowe.  The crews started on Monday afternoon and they worked Monday through Wednesday, took off for turkey day and finished up on Friday and Saturday.  The crews worked in groups of 4 with 2 sawyers and 2 buckers (brush removers).  They were able to go over all of the trails once and got most clearing done.

      Chris and Nancy Riehl, crew leaders for Trail F, along with Greg Hersberger, Jamie Cardiff and Chris Lowe, brought their own skid loaders down to repair the trail with Chris and Nancy putting down the geotech.  Earlier, Jamie and Kyle Farr had worked a day with the skid loaders repairing another spot on trail F.  The ITRA supplied over 100 tons of stone.  This is a part of the grant we have from Brown County Soil and Water Conservation.  Yvette, Gracie and Taylor Rollins, Brett Erwood,

Dave and Erin Maksymczak and Steve Auth worked on the crossing on Trail 7 and Bruce and Jody Weldy used their ATV’s to haul the stone from the top of the hill to the creek crossing.   Erin Maksymczak, Gracie and Taylor Rollins later that day picked up 3 big bags of trash out on the trail and in the campground.

      Scotty Eskridge and Dave and Erin Maksymzcak did a fantastic job getting the shelter house ready for the wonderful pitch-in dinner and meeting.  A generator was used to power several strings of lights.  A roaring fireplace on each end of the shelter house made it very cozy. Now for the Pitch-in dinner!  As usual we had plenty of food.  We had ham, barbecue, slow cookers filled with a variety of casseroles, vegetables, rolls, bread, pies, cakes and fruit.  You name it,  we had it!  It was very good eating and festive.  Brett Erwood took lots of  pictures.  Greg made the ham and the barbecue and it was delicious.

      After dinner the Board of Directors meeting took place.  After the meeting members stayed for the tear down of plastic and packing up all of the supplies that were used.
