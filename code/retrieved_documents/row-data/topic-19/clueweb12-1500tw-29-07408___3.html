Who’s your daddy – Mac or PC?

Posted on' August 3rd, 2010 by agragati

 

This is one of the most famous rivalries known to man. Which is better, the PC or the Mac? Of course we are talking about the personal computer versus the Macintosh. The two rivals have been known to use advertisements that poke jibes at one another. The rivalry has even fuelled websites that deal with only the competition and the advances the two products are making.

A Mac is a PC

But what most people forget that in the Mac vs. PC war it is the individual operating systems loaded on the hardware that is actually being compared and not the physical Mac or PC. When people talk or ask about ‘which is better, Mac or PC?’ they are actually asking whichoperating system , i.e. Mac OS and any of the Microsoft products like Windows XP, is better.

For the sake of simplicity let us only look at the bad side of both systems, since every user on one system or the other obviously knows what’s good about his or her system of choice. So, without further ado:

What’s wrong with Mac:

-          Let’s be fair and give credit where it is due. Windows is the most widely used OS on the planet. And even though Apple is making great progress it still has a long way to go before it catches up with Microsoft.

-          Somewhat in relation to the first point, there aren’t nearly as much applications that run on Mac OS as on Windows.

-          Compatibility is one weakness with the Mac OS. While you can plug in almost any bit of hardware or gadget on Windows system and have it instantly recognized, with a Mac it’s a bit more complicated.

-          Gaming is a pleasure on a Windows, enough said! Integrating Direct X directly into the hardware was a revolutionary idea.

What’s wrong with Windows:

-          You cannot install Mac OS on a PC with Windows on it. But the reverse is possible – you can install Windows on a machine running Mac OS, and even get updates from Apple.

-          It’s ugly. One can go out on a limb and say that not very much has changed since the days of Windows ’95. And you can’t help but think of the ‘Ugly Duckling’ when you have to talk about Mac vs. Windows.

-          One word: ‘security’. Just mention that word around a Windows user and see the worry lines get deeper in his face. The problem is that Windows can be made as secure as a Mac OS, but you just have to know how to do it.

-          Everyone knows that the support at Apple is great. Whether at a store (you don’t have to buy it from them) or using the old online techs, you have a great net to fall back on. At Windows, not so good.
