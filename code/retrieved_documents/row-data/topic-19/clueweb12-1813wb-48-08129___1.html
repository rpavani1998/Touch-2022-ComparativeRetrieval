PC vs. Mac: Which is the Right One for You?

Posted by:

Posted on Monday, July 18, 2011

Which is better, PC or Mac?

The debate on whether what system is better has been going on for more than a decade now. Some have been arguing based on its features and specifications while many debates have been obscured by senseless personal attacks.

Based on the arguments found in most online message boards, the long-lasting debate has been reduced to this: advocates of Windows PCs are ignorant and clueless about computers and their features, and Mac fans are elitists or social climbers.


Photo by cecilgene

But looking at it objectively, not everyone who buys computing devices is incompetent in choosing or a member of a small group of rich brats. Consumers are smart. They know what they need and what is important to them.

So for those people who want to do their research first before deciding what system to buy, read along to see where PCs and Macs stand.

Cost

PCs are generally cheaper compared to Mac computers. You can buy a brand new fully set up desktop PC for more or less PhP 25,000.00. But iMacs are not available in that price range as the most affordable model costs around PhP 55,000.

Aside from the expensive Mac unit, its parts and services are also expensive. Macs have fewer proprietary parts and their internal drives are not user-replaceable. Mac’s service and repairs can cost even beyond the unit’s cost. So far, Apple’s extended warranty, AppleCare Protection Plan, is the most expensive in the industry.

Selection

The best thing about Windows PCs is the variety of choices. They come in different designs, colors and sizes, and they differ in specifications and features. iMacs, however, come in few basic variants.

Design

Macs look more stylish and sleek than most PCs and thus, it appeals very much to the consumers who are looking for computers which are more modern and classy in appearance.

Operating System

A powerful argument in favor of buying Mac is its operating system. Unlike Windows XP, Vista, or Windows 7, Mac’s OS, OS X 10.6 Snow Leopard, is more consistent, and less sluggish.

Security

Since majority of computer users use Windows, most of the known viruses, Trojans, and other malicious softwares and applications are designed to attack only Windows PCs. And even though there are now some virus attacks on Macs, they are still more secure [even without antivirus software] compared to a Windows PC that runs an antivirus program.

Whether you are using an iMac or a Windows PC, all the basic features, such as the web, camera, software, printer, and other hardware, are fine in both systems. The only things that would really matter and greatly affect your decision are your needs, preference, and more importantly, your budget.
