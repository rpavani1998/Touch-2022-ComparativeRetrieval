POSTAL PRINT FOLIO

The SW Region has three postal print folios (general) and one Black and White Digital in circulation now.  Each has a maximum of 12 members who circulate prints (no bigger than A4 and unmounted) round their group and comment on each other’s images. There is no limit as to subject, and whether the image is a mono from a wet darkroom or has been heavily “photo shopped” in colour is immaterial.  Members benefit from friendly critique and enjoy seeing a variety of different pictures. (We do not score images like some folios). Also, meeting fellow circle members at Regional events is a good way of getting to know them better. Currently there are spaces for members to join a folio. If you are interested or would like to know more please contact me:

Will Cheung

Will has enjoyed working in the photographic press for 25 years and is currently the editor for Advanced Photography Magazine. He provides workshops for Jessops training academy, Leica, Digital Depot and Nikon training. He also works with Wellshot imaging, a company offering workshops and academies in North Wales and North West England. His photographic interests are very wide (People, events, scenics, nature, travel) and there is no subject he does not enjoy. Will is a Fellow of the RPS and sits on the distinctions panel for the applied category.www.williamcheung.co.uk

 

Nick Meers

Nick shoots editorial, advertising and stock, landscapes, locations, gardens, architecture, panoramics and the details of life and light. Nick’s panoramics are taken with an adapted camera that sometimes needs 2 tripods; a panoramic format 6x17cm format film holder bolted onto the rear of a Linhof TechniKarden view camera. Nick has been commissioned to shoot over 30 books and images have been used for many magazines. He has a long list of clients. Nick gives master classes, private tuition, workshops in the UK and USA. He has judged various prestigious competitions including the Travel Photographer of the Year. www.nickmeers.com

WEBSITE 

It was refreshing to get images for the website from new members following the email sent out at the back end on 2011. I now have a good supply to refresh the images when required. Due to a good response in field trip images I have had to take down the older ones to accommodate all that we get from field trips. It was said originally we would leave field trip images on the site for a year but due to numbers this has been cut down to about 3 or 4 field trips on view. Please do keep the field trip images coming as it shows others what they have missed, but please send them promptly after the event in order they are fresh. We also need news, this can be exhibitions, events which are photography based, be they camera club or otherwise, plus any news which can be shared with members.

Acceptances into RPS exhibitions are also good news to be shared, we can display some of the images, and especially those that win ribbons or medals but generally the only people who can send these are the authors so do not be shy.

We do of course reserve the right to veto any content, which we feel is not suitable, this generally takes the form of being circulated to some of the committee members who respond with their thoughts.

If anyone has any queries or suggestions for the website please contact myself, or Jenny Leathes and we will help you if we can.

If anybody would like to contribute please email me jpeg files, ideally 465 x 310 pixels at 72 dpi (I will resize if necessary). Images display better in landscape, but if you want to send portrait images these need to be no bigger than 310 pixels high and preferably on a canvas 465 pixels wide, and the canvas can then be a colour of your choice.

Please also have a look at the website the day before an event, especially field trips, to see if there are any changes of venue or time.

Mick Medley, Regional Webmaster

Programme

Fri. 12th Dinner 7.00pm, 9 to 10 pm talk from a SW Region member (travel
