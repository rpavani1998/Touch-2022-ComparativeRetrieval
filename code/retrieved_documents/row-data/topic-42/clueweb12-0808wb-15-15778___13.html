There is a lot of hate for the Tundra, and there are a few reasons why.. Right off the top, you are always going to have the “Remember Pearl Harbor” factor.. As far as I`m concerned, I would love a Pearl Harbor edition Tundra, bullet holes n` all.
  Then you have the “real” Americans that just have to “buy American” because buying an American car is good for America.. Thats why you bought your Dodge that was made by skilled Mexican workers. Where did your money go? To the company and the workers.. Just the people that needed the money right? Help save us from the recession, Don`t buy that Toyota! It`s not good for the skilled American craftsman that build it.
 I drive a 2000 Tundra SR5 4x2 V-8 with 189,000 miles on the clock. I just had the 3rd timing belt put in, along with a new water pump. Besides the timing belt and water pump, the only other things that have needed to be fixed since my father bought it in `99 are the starter @145,000 and overdrive & torque converter @ 138,000. (The reason for only od & tq converter is my trans guy said the other 3 gears were “like new”). Why put in a new trans when you don`t need it?  That`s the maintenance record so far, besides oil changes, 2 sets of spark plugs, numerous trans services (every 25,000), 4 sets of shocks, 1 set of sway bar links and poly bushings up front, 3 sets of rotors & pads, the drums & shoes are original.
   From the factory, the suspension was a little lacking I thought.. So I installed Firestone Ride Rite airbags in the back before the truck was 2 years old. The front end was always way too soft for my liking, especially with 500 lbs or more in the back.. I found a deal in Performance products on a set of H&R progressive springs and 4 Bilstein 5100 shock absorbers.. Had them put on @ 184,000 miles.  Suspension problems cured..  
 Here I am now, less than 6 months away from 200,000 miles and I can see yet another reason why so many people just hate the Tundra`s  friggin`guts! Stories like mine… They wish they could get 180,000 miles out of a water pump, a front wheel bearing, alternator, transmission, or a rear end. They should have bought a Tundra.
  Can you believe those idiots at Toyota!? They have to copy everybody else! Dodge makes a 5.7 liter Hemi, Toyota makes a 5.7 liter that outperforms it and gets better gas mileage.. Blah blah.. Toyota copied Dodge, blah blah.. Toyota copied Ford with the outboard shock absorbers, blah blah. How about the fact that the domestics are now copying Toyota and Honda with VVT? You don`t hear about that much.. What about Dodge copying Toyota`s 5.7 liter with VVT? Haven`t heard much about that yet.. Doubt I will..  
  Bottom line is: There is a lot of people who do not like the Tundra, most of them don`t have a leg to stand on because they have never owned one or drove one for 200,000 trouble free miles. Maybe they don`t have a leg to stand on because they lost it in Pearl Harbor, either way, I`m going to run my Tundra into the ground. And when I send it off to scrap, I`ll be right back at the Toyota dealer buying another Tundra.

In the past 6 years I have owned a Chevy K1500 (Silverado, sorry), a Nissan Titan and now a 2008 Ford F-150.  I also owned a T-100 way back in the day before the Chevy.

All three were great trucks, honestly.  I had no complaints with any of them.  The Chevy was a great all around truck, did what it was asked altho' the payload capacity was not all that great.

The Titan had an engine/transmission combination that put a lot of power to the pavement, and while the ride was a bit harsh it, again, did what was expected of it.

My F-150 has a good ride, decent power (5.4), handles well despite feeling like a tank.  So far it too has done all that has been asked of it.

My PERSONAL preference of the three is the Ford simply because it really is built to take a beating, not to drag race.  I have not tried a new Silverado, or a new Dodge.  When I bought my Chevy I had not been impressed with the Dodge (did test drive one) but I hear the previous generation (to the current coming out) was quite good.

My mileage in ALL of my trucks has ranged from 16 MPG to 20 MPG, depending on what I was doing on any given tank.

As far as owning/driving foreign vehicles... my wife is a Toyota lover.  All of her cars have been Toyotas... and she drives them to the ground.  Starting with her first car, a Tercel, then a Rav 4, now an Avalon.  Never had a problem with any of them, and I have never had a problem with any of my trucks.

Honestly I think that for LIGHT DUTY any of the trucks in that class will not out right fail in their role.  The Titan and Tundra are both built here... so Americans are paid to build them.  My Ford was built in Kansas.

Anyway, I just think that NONE of the trucks are junk and will all serve their purpose.
