Best Trucks for the Bucks

By Tom Wilson of MSN Autos

With so many new vehicles on the market, picking just one is a challenge, especially if you're looking for a newpickup truck. Not only are there multiple manufacturers, each with more than one model to choose from, but each pickup comes in a bewildering variety ofcab styles, wheelbases, trim levels, engine options, transmission choices and 2- or 4-wheel drive. To help narrow the search, we've collected some of our bang-for-the-truck-buck favorites. You'll find a range of offerings here, from compacts to full-size to heavy-duty models, each selected with an eye toward balancing capacity and performance with cost.

Read:  Pickup Trucks, Tow to Tow

Best Trucks for the Bucks

Ford Ranger

Price range: $17,935 to $26,025
Ford is discontinuing the Ranger after the 2011 model year, so it might seem strange to put it on a 10-best list. This compact truck is a hoary old thing — basic and lacking frills, but tough as nails and light on the checkbook. Kept simple with an anemic 2.3-liter 4-banger engine and a 5-speed manual transmission, it's an easygoing runabout that'll tote any sort of bulky or dirty gear. In fact, it's perfect for any odd job and can be had for just a bit more than $12,000 on sale.

Bing: Ford Ranger

Best Trucks for the Bucks

Nissan Frontier

Price range: $17,540 to $29,820
When there are no more Rangers, Nissan'sFrontier will lead the entry-level compact truck market nicely. True, up hills and in modern traffic it's lethargic in 4-cylinder form. But step up to the V6 engine and it rockets right along. Like theRanger, the Nissan is cheap to buy, easy on gas and handy to drive in town or through tight spots on the ranch, and it is a decent off-roader. Unlike the Ranger, though, it's definitely a little larger and more comfortable inside. It's also available as a crew cab if four doors are a must-have feature.

Bing Images: Nissan Frontier

Best Trucks for the Bucks

Toyota Tacoma

Price range: $16,365 to $25,525
If our first two trucks are notable mainly for their low price, this midsize offering makes the grade on capability, features and reputation. TheTacoma boasts excellent build quality and a fantastic resale value that make it a great buy. But the Tacoma's reputation as the best midsize pickup is genuinely earned on both performance and quality; it's a comfortable, stylish truck that doesn't shy from real work. We'd opt for the 4.0-liter V6 engine for the oomph expected in a midsize truck, as well as for providing the up to 6,500-pound tow rating.

Compare: Toyota Tacoma vs. Ford Ranger vs. Nissan Frontier

Best Trucks for the Bucks

Dodge Dakota

Price range: $22,755 to $33,180
Just as parent company Chrysler invented the minivan,Dodge invented the midsize pickup with the Dakota more than 20 years ago, and the experience shows. Perfectly sized and very stylish, the Dakota also indulges Dodge's habit of not sparing the power. Fitted with the optional 4.7-liter V8 engine, the Dakota is a torquey powerhouse that takes top honors in payload capacity and offers up to a 7,000-pound tow rating; most midsize trucks tow around 4,700 pounds. Just be careful not to option the Dakota too far into full-size territory, which is easy to do.

Bing: Dodge Dakota Accessories

Best Trucks for the Bucks

Dodge Ram 1500

Price range: $20,610 to $42,650
When the Dakota isn't enough, it's time to consider the first full-size pickup on our list, theRam 1500. A real workhorse — as suggested by its clean, bold styling — the Ram is financially superior with its smaller 4.7-liter V8 engine, but it's far more fun and capable with the 390-horsepower 5.7-liter multidisplacement HEMI. It tows far more that way and gets the same mileage. But the Ram's hidden secret is its coil-spring rear suspension, which gives it a supple ride and precise handling, and there's still enough truck muscle left for durability and rough duty.

Video: Dodge Ram 1500

Best Trucks for the Bucks

Ford F-150 XLT Custom

Price range: $27,710 to $33,895
Class-leading in almost every respect and boasting all-new engines, the 2011Ford F-150 lineup is particularly strong, but the XLT Custom is purpose-built for the bang-for-the-buck buyer. Nicely equipped with midlevel trim, a 4x2 driveline and the fuel-sipping, 3.7-liter 302-horsepower V6 engine, the XLT Custom can tow 6,100 pounds and looks good doing it, with its chrome grille and tailpipe, 18-inch wheels and unique cloth trim. The Custom option is also available with the SuperCrew and SuperCab trims as well.

Read:  Keep on Truckin'

Best Trucks for the Bucks

Ford F-150 3.5-liter EcoBoost

Price range: $24,465 to $48,795
Ford is onto something with its combination of gasoline direct-injection and dual turbocharging. They call it EcoBoost, and with the 2011 F-150's new 3.5-liter V6 engine, it delivers 420 lb-ft of diesel-like torque, 365 horsepower and highway mileage into the mid-20s. Able to tow up to 11,300 pounds, the EcoBoost V6 is a $1,750 premium over the nonturbocharged base V6, which is far less than what an equivalent diesel would have cost. Best yet, the power is instant and quiet. This truck is definitely worth a look if you're looking for a true workhorse.

Read:  Ford Expands EcoBoost Engine Rollout

Best Trucks for the Bucks

Chevrolet Silverado 1500

Price range: $20,850 to $41,775
Robust, durable engines that aren't technically overboard — read "simple" — are a hallmark of Chevy trucks. Available anywhere in the U.S., priced right and often available in numerous boots-and-jeans versions where other makes emphasize suburban style, Chevy'sSilverado series rides a wave of deep-seated popularity. If pricing is paramount, the 4.3-liter V6 engine is as proven and reliable as the sunrise, but well down on power. We'd concentrate on the 5.3-liter V8 for much better power and essentially the same fuel economy.Chevy also offers a hybrid, but bang for the buck is last on its list of attributes.

Video: Chevrolet Silverado 1500

Best Trucks for the Bucks

Chevrolet Silverado 3500 HD

Price range: $31,760 to $44,950
When it comes to the bruising, heavy-duty pickups, it's truly tough to pick a favorite, although we'll say the pricey diesel options are still the way to go, because even though they cost bucks, they have incredible bang.Chevy, Dodge and Ford each make one — the imports haven't thought this big yet — and each has been recently updated. We like the smooth, inline 6-cylinder Cummins engine in the Dodge, but Chevy'sSilverado HD goes toe-to-toe or better on price and offers 47 more horsepower and 115 more lb-ft of torque in its new 6.6-liter V8.

Read:  2011 Chevrolet Silverado HD — First Drive

Best Trucks for the Bucks

Ford F-350 Super Duty

Price range: $28,740 to $50,440
Leaning more to the performance end of our bang-for-the-buck equation, we arrive at the ultimate in pickup trucks, theSuper Duty packing the all-new 6.7-liter Ford diesel V8 engine. We're taking a bit of a gamble here, as this is Ford's first in-house diesel, but its 400 horsepower and 800 lb-ft of torque carry the ultimate bragging rights, not to mention just about anything tied to its trailer hitch. Impressive trim packages, advanced electronics and generally superior detailing are there to satisfy those who find quality is nearly always the best buy.

Bing: Ford F-350 Super Duty
