10 Most Fuel-Efficient Workhorses

By the editors of Popular Mechanics

Does your truck get 27 miles per gallon? The day-in, day-out contractor will scoff at such a question — as he drives away in the big, powerful heavy-dutydiesel truck that he needs to make a living. But for those of us who want a truck body — still able to haul plenty of stuff — with less power and a lot bettergas mileage, there are plenty of trucks out there for you. Here are the top 10 most fuel efficient 2011 trucks.

Popular Mechanics: The Top 10 Cars of 2011: Auto Excellence Awards

10 Most Fuel-Efficient Workhorses

2011 Dodge Ram 1500 Pickup 2W

Base Price: $20,810
The Numbers: 20 mpg hwy, 14 mpg city, 16 mpg overall
The Tradeoff: With one of the only V8 engines on this list, there is actually no difference in fuel economy between the Ram 1500's lesser 215-hp V6 and either the flex-fuel 4.7L V8 or the powerhouse 5.7L Hemi V8 (good for 390 hp). Unfortunately, there's still a significant gap in price, with the top V8 model starting well into the $30Ks.

Bing Images: Dodge Ram 1500

10 Most Fuel-Efficient Workhorses

2011 Honda Ridgeline Truck 4WD

Base Price: $29,150
The Numbers: 20 mpg hwy, 15 mpg city, 17 mpg overall
The Tradeoff: Despite its 4WD drivetrain, theHonda Ridgeline is still the least truck-like offering here, but offers the most civilized ride, thanks to itsOdyssey minivan platform. When we tested the Ridgeline, we found that its pleasant highway manners were a slight consolation for its sub-par abilities as a utility or off-road truck.

Popular Mechanics: Heavy-Duty Diesel Pickup Comparison Test

10 Most Fuel-Efficient Workhorses

2011 Dodge Dakota Pickup 2WD

Base Price: $23,210
The Numbers: 20 mpg hwy, 15 mpg city, 17 mpg overall
The Tradeoff: We haven't tested the 2011Dakota yet, but it is much the same middle-of-the-road truck that it was last year. The midsize Dodge pickup features a 3.7L Magnum V6 as standard equipment, with a 4.7L V8 being the more enticing option, albeit one that would knock the Dakota off this list.

Bing: Dodge Dakota

10 Most Fuel-Efficient Workhorses

2011 Toyota Tundra 2WD

Base Price: $24,435
The Numbers: 20 mpg hwy, 16 mpg city, 18 mpg overall
The Tradeoff: Like nearly everything here, the 2011Tundra made it into the top ten with its smallest engine and no 4WD. Luckily, the entry-level 4.0L V6 received a boost in power for 2011, now good for 270 hp and 278 lb-ft of torque.

Popular Mechanics: Lamborghini Brings Back the V12

10 Most Fuel-Efficient Workhorses

2011 Chevrolet Colorado 2WD (GMC Canyon 2WD)

Base Price: $17,145 ($17,155)
The Numbers: 25 mpg hwy, 18 mpg city, 21 mpg overall
The Tradeoff: The Chevrolet Colorado and its cousin the GMC Canyon carry over into 2011 largely unchanged, much as they have since their introduction in 2004. The base 185-hp four-cylinder engine is more powerful than its direct competition, but it's also larger and thirstier.

Watch Video:  2010 Chevrolet Colorado

10 Most Fuel-Efficient Workhorses

2011 Suzuki Equator 2WD

Base Price: $17,595
The Numbers: 23 mpg hwy, 19 mpg city, 21 mpg overall
The Tradeoff: Like the Nissan Frontier on which it's based, the Suzuki Equator is the underdog player in the entry-level pickup truck arena, although its 7-year/100,000-mile warranty is definitely a plus.

10 Most Fuel-Efficient Workhorses

2011 Nissan Frontier 2WD

Base Price: $18,200
The Numbers: 23 mpg hwy, 19 mpg city, 21 mpg overall
The Tradeoff: The Tennessee-builtNissan Frontier has enjoyed moderate success since its introduction in 1997, perhaps in part thanks to its attempt at rugged "Tonka Truck" styling, and despite its relative lack of standard options compared to the competition.

Compare: Nissan Frontier vs. Ford Ranger vs. Chevrolet Colorado

10 Most Fuel-Efficient Workhorses

2011 Chevrolet Silverado 1500 Hybrid 2WD (GMC Sierra 15 Hybrid 2WD)

Base Price: $38,725 ($39,095)
The Numbers: 23 mpg hwy, 20 mpg city, 21 mpg overall
The Tradeoff: The Silverado/Sierra duo are the only hybrids on the list, and feature the same two-mode hybrid transmission and cylinder-deactivation 6.0L V8 as the hybrid Tahoe/Yukon SUVs.

Popular Mechanics: How to Safely Work on Hybrid Electric Vehicles

10 Most Fuel-Efficient Workhorses

2011 Toyota Tacoma 2WD

Base Price: $16,365
The Numbers: 25 mpg hwy, 21 mpg city, 22 mpg overall
The Tradeoff: After moving production to San Antonio last year, the biggest change fromToyota on the 2011 Tacoma comes in the form of a price increase, although at least a/c is now one of the new standard equipment features. It's still one of the most affordable pickups out there, although its base 159-hp engine is bested by much of the competition.

Compare: Toyota Tacoma vs. Suzuki Equator vs. GMC Canyon

10 Most Fuel-Efficient Workhorses

2011 Ford Ranger 2WD

Base Price: $18,160
The Numbers: 27 mpg hwy, 22 mpg city, 24 mpg overall
The Tradeoff: With a combined 24 mpg, the rear-wheel-driveRanger takes first place here. Regrettably, however, the all-new (and significantly updated) 2011 Ranger that recently debuted at the Sydney Auto Show will not be seen on U.S. roads, at least not yet. We make do with a slightly updated version of last year's Ranger.
