4 Perfect Pear Recipes

February 9th, 2012

Maple Magic Pears with Cheddar

There aren’t many fruits that are associated with winter, but pears might just be one of them. Varieties such as D’Anjou pears are ready to be picked in the fall, and then take four to six weeks to ripen, and can be kept in cold storage for months. You can find lots of delicious pears in markets now, and many of them are lovely to be eaten on their own or added to salads.

Try these four recipes for more ways to enjoy winter pears!

Maple Magic Pears with Cheddar
These are magical for the fact that they only have four ingredients. They come together quickly, and make for a lovely dessert.

Winter Greens Salad with Pancetta, Pears and Pecans
Hearty greens are sturdy enough to hold up well to this warm honey tarragon vinaigrette dressing. Feel free to switch the pears to apple, pancetta to bacon or pecans to almonds in this perfect cold-weather entrée salad.

Blue Cheese and Pear Salmon

Blue Cheese and Pear Salmon
It may sound unusual, but a little Canadian blue and pear make this salmon a must try.

Roast Chicken and Squash with Spiced Pear Chutney
There’s nothing more scrumptious than the combination of sweet and savory.

Ingredient Primer: Jicama

February 1st, 2012

Richard B's Vegetarian Tacos

If a radish and a lumpy potato got together and had a baby, it would look something like jicama. Jicama is a root vegetable, and it’s also known as a Mexican yam or a Mexican turnip. Not surprisingly, it’s very popular in Mexican cooking, and is also used regularly in southeast Asian cooking.

Jicama boasts lots of dietary fibre, vitamins A, B and C as well as some calcium, and it can be eaten raw or cooked. It can be mashed like a potato, thrown into salads or added to stir fries. It has a very mild sweet and nutty flavour, and its texture isn’t unlike that of water chestnuts.

If you’ve never cooked with jicama, give it a shot with one of these four recipes.

Richard B’s Vegetarian Tacos
These no-cook tacos are a zesty spin on international entertaining. Thinly-sliced jicama rounds are used in place of taco shells!

Seared Halibut with White Summer Salad
Don’t limit yourself—any fish is delicious with this salad. And we know it’s not summer, but the salad features jicama, fennel and white asparagus. While not all of these are in season (and we’re fans of seasonal eating), they can be found in grocery stores right now.

Orange and Avocado Salsa

Orange and Avocado Salsa
Serve this refreshing salsa with lightly grilled flatbread.

Quick Fish Tacos with Lime Sour Cream
Fresh and flavourful, this quick meal is nutritious and fun for the whole family. Ingredients can be set out on the table allowing everyone a chance to assemble tacos to their personal taste. Other terrific taco toppers would be diced avocado, jicama or radish slices or chopped green onion.

4 Healthier Super Bowl Snacks

January 30th, 2012

Crispy Quinoa, Lime and Chipotle-Crusted Chicken Strips

After the major holidays, Super Bowl Sunday might just be one of the greatest food events of the year. Even if you’re not a football fan, the food alone is enough to get anyone excited about the game.

But, like most of our major holidays, the food served isn’t exactly health-check approved. Chicken wings, nachos with cheese sauce, pizza, beer… it’s not exactly a healthfest. But you can lighten up some Super Bowl favourites with these delicious options. Not all of these are lower in fat, but they all deliver more health per serving, whether it be whole wheat ingredients, more protein, more healthy fats or less sodium than their store-bought cousins. Game on!

Crispy Quinoa, Lime and Chipotle-Crusted Chicken Fingers
Think outside the usual quinoa salad box and this fabulous ancient grain can offer endless recipe possibilities. Keep cooked quinoa available in the fridge for extra quick meal prep. Crunchy carrots sticks make a delightful accompaniment.

Whole Wheat Nachos with Corn
This spin on nachos still packs a cheesy punch, but whole wheat is a healthier base than traditional nacho chips.

Avocado Mango Salsa

Homemade Whole Wheat and Beer Pizza
This pizza has been known to disappear off plates in seconds. Be creative with your toppings and use what’s in your fridge.

Avocado Mango Salsa
The mango adds a sweet and lively twist to this spicy salsa. Delicious with chicken or fish, or on its own with soft corn tortillas. Plus, when you make it at home, you get to control how much sodium goes in.
