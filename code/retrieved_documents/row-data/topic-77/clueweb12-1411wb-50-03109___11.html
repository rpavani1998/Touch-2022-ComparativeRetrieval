6 Oscar-Worthy Party Foods

Tuesday, February 21st, 2012

Baked Mushroom-Topped Brie

The Oscars are this weekend, and whether you’re a movie buff or not, the awards show is always a good excuse for a party. It’s also a good excuse to try new recipes that speak to the glamour of the evening—skip the nachos and wings (unless you love them) and try some decidedly fancier fare.

Here are six tasty treats that you can serve to keep your guests satisfied from the red carpet coverage all the way to the Best Picture announcement.

Baked Mushroom-Topped Brie
Prepare and chill the mushroom topping to have on hand for the big night. Baking and serving on oven-proof platter keeps the cheese warm while eating. Serve with sliced baguette or your favourite crackers.

Bacon, Caramelized Onion and Rosemary Pizza with Blue Cheese
Sliced into squares or slivers, this is a great party appetizer. Onions and bacon can both be cooked in advance and refrigerated, allowing the pizza to be assembled in minutes. If you want to make it sound fancier, replace the word “pizza” with “flatbread”.

Sweet Potato Almond Chipotle Soup

Sweet Potato and Almond Chipotle Soup
This soup is a perfect balance of sweet from the sweet potato, earthy from the almonds and heat from the smoky chipotle—good for you too! Serve them in tiny servings to make them easier to eat around the TV.

Almond-Crusted Chevre and Grape Truffles
This is a deceptively simple make ahead hors d’oeuvre or a great activity for an interactive party. It’s a substantial nibble, think traditional cheese platter with fruits and almonds, all in one bite!

Savoury Strawberry and Pecan Truffles
One bite into this decadent morsel and you’ll be asking for more. Velvety goat cheese blended with the sweetness of strawberries, crunch of pecans and then drizzled with a decadent, artisan honey.

Little Spring Cakes
Filled with fruit, these little individual cakes are the perfect addition to a party spread for those who like a little sweet at the end of an evening. Serve them when you bring out the tea and coffee.

4 Healthier Super Bowl Snacks

Monday, January 30th, 2012

Crispy Quinoa, Lime and Chipotle-Crusted Chicken Strips

After the major holidays, Super Bowl Sunday might just be one of the greatest food events of the year. Even if you’re not a football fan, the food alone is enough to get anyone excited about the game.

But, like most of our major holidays, the food served isn’t exactly health-check approved. Chicken wings, nachos with cheese sauce, pizza, beer… it’s not exactly a healthfest. But you can lighten up some Super Bowl favourites with these delicious options. Not all of these are lower in fat, but they all deliver more health per serving, whether it be whole wheat ingredients, more protein, more healthy fats or less sodium than their store-bought cousins. Game on!

Crispy Quinoa, Lime and Chipotle-Crusted Chicken Fingers
Think outside the usual quinoa salad box and this fabulous ancient grain can offer endless recipe possibilities. Keep cooked quinoa available in the fridge for extra quick meal prep. Crunchy carrots sticks make a delightful accompaniment.

Whole Wheat Nachos with Corn
This spin on nachos still packs a cheesy punch, but whole wheat is a healthier base than traditional nacho chips.

Avocado Mango Salsa

Homemade Whole Wheat and Beer Pizza
This pizza has been known to disappear off plates in seconds. Be creative with your toppings and use what’s in your fridge.

Avocado Mango Salsa
The mango adds a sweet and lively twist to this spicy salsa. Delicious with chicken or fish, or on its own with soft corn tortillas. Plus, when you make it at home, you get to control how much sodium goes in.

4 Resolution-Approved Sweets

Thursday, January 12th, 2012

Strawberry Tiramisu

You’ve made your healthy resolutions for 2012, and now you just need a little help sticking to them? No problem.

Here are four sweet treats you can make, serve and enjoy without any guilt attached.

Strawberry Tiramisu
Creamy and velvety, this spinoff of the famous Italian tiramisu is less fattening, easier to make and sure to be a hit!

Cinnamon Apple Chips
Crisp, oven baked apple chips with a hint of cinnamon-y-sweetness offer a nutritious solution for snack time.

Sweet "Puppy Chow"

Chocolate Surprise Muffins
Make these rich Chocolate Surprise Muffins packed with zucchini as a special treat. These muffins are as healthy as they are decadent.

Sweet “Puppy Chow” Snack Mix
This snack mix is a nutritious take on the sweet party mix often referred to as “puppy chow.” With less butter, less sugar and more fibre and antioxidants, this version doesn’t have to be reserved just for parties.

5 Tasty Movie-Night Snacks

Friday, October 21st, 2011

Cheese and Seed Savoury Cookies

A new study has proven what most of have known for years: cold weather can make you want to curl up indoors with a great movie (particularlyromantic comedies).

So whip up a batch of something delicious (whether sweet or salty), grab a blanket and have a solid night in. Here are our top movie-night snack foods:

Cheese and Seed Savoury Cookies
These savory cookies, perfect for any time of day, are so delicious they won’t last long!

Taco Mexican Snack Mix
This is a great little snack. Not sure what to do with the left over taco seasoning you have? Sprinkle it over popcorn and place it on the snack table next to the Taco Mexican mix for another delicious snack.

Spiced Chocolate Almonds
Serve these spicy delicious almonds alone, or mix them with 2 to 3 cups of popcorn. This tasty treat is the perfect mix of salty and sweet.

Chocolate-Dipped Pretzel Rods

Chocolate Dipped Pretzel Rods
Chocolate covered pretzels are hard for many to resist. Add a rainbow of candy coated sprinkles and this simple, kid-friendly recipe will become a household favourite. An equal amount of white chocolate can be substituted in this recipe, if desired.

Rosemary Oven-Roasted Potato Chips
Get creative and enjoy making your own batch of delicious rosemary potato chips.

8 Cookies You’ll Crave

Monday, September 19th, 2011

Almond Butter Cookies

We love cookies. They’re good to pack in lunches, good at tea time, good for a snack and good for the soul. Whether you’re into the classics (chocolate chip, oatmeal, etc.,) or are looking to try something new (potato chips and pecans?), try one of our eight favourite cookies below.

Almond Butter Cookies
Delicious and buttery melt-in-your-mouth cookies.

Iron Skillet Chocolate Chip Cookie
A classic favourite cookie gets a grown-up makeover with a new cooking surface and some sophisticated salt. The result is a gooey and soft middle with crispy bottom and edges.

Potato Chip Pecan Crunch Cookies
Don’t tell anyone there are crushed potato chips in the mix so that all they will care about is the buttery goodness.

Raspberry Almond Macarons
This isn’t the coconut macaroon many people are familiar with. Instead, it’s a lovely, light-as-a-feather French sandwich cookie, which starts with puffy wafers of ground almonds, confectioners’ sugar and egg white. This version is sealed with a rich, pink, raspberry-flavoured buttercream.

No-Bake Chocolate Cookies
These cookies can be made in just three simple steps.

Oatmeal Cranberry Flaxseed Cookies
These tasty and healthy cookies are a real favourite with the kids! Perfect for lunch boxes.

Confetti Meringues
Perfect for a party!

Jane’s Killer Chocolate Cookies
You truly cannot get much more chocolate into a single cookie.
