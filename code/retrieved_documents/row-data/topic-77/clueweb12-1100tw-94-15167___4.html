Chicken Fricassee with Cream



Tweet
Share

Get it all on this Recipe...


Servings 4 Preparation Time
  • 35 minutes
  • Cooking: 40 minutes

Cooking Time 35 minutes
Recommended Wine Gevrey Chambertin, Pommard Ingredients 1 chicken (about 4.4 lbs.), 14 ½ tablespoons butter, 2 cup double cream (40-45% fat content), 12 baby onions (cut into 4 â€“ 5 chunks each), 3 garlic cloves, mushrooms, 1 1/4 cups white wine, thyme, 1 laurel leaf, pepper, salt Preparation Instructions Cut and prepare the chicken and place it in a saucepan with butter, onions, garlic, mushrooms, laurel leaf, and thyme. Season with salt and pepper. Let the chicken slices caramelize. Add white wine and cream. Remove the chicken pieces and strain the sauce. Pour the sauce over the chicken just before serving. Restaurant Name Chez Georges Blanc
Chef Name Georges Blanc

Chicken Fricassee with Cream

Chicken breasts are loved for their texture and versatility in cooking. Make them a part of any dish - bake, grill, boil or fry. Chicken breasts are quite adaptable and can be used in variety of dishes, ranging from easy, quick, healthy recipes to exotic ones. Chicken breast is the leanest part of the chicken and is therefore healthier. However, chicken with the skin doubles the fat content. Hence, chicken is best skinned before cooking. Lean chicken breast has the least amount of fat, cholesterol and saturated fatty acids. It is a good source of Vitamins A and E.

Â 

Chicken breast should be cooked until it reaches an internal temperature of 165 degrees. This will help in avoiding the risk of food borne illness. Chicken should be stored in the coldest section of your refrigerator. Handle the raw chicken carefully. Do not touch other food items while handling raw chicken and wash your hands thoroughly after you are done. Always refrigerate the marinated chicken as it tends to spoil quickly when exposed to heat. Even the cooked chicken should be kept inside the fridge if you are not consuming it immediately. Chicken breasts taste great when they are moist and tender. Overcooking may make the chicken breasts dry, tough and stringy. While using the dry heat mode such as baking, roasting, grilling, sauteing, cook on a high temperature for a short period. Under moist mode of cooking, longer period of time and low flame ensures the best chicken breasts.
