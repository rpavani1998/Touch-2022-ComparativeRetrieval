Book update

  • Dec. 14th, 2009 at 10:47 AM

Book List

Recently Read: Venus by Bova

I give it a 3/5. Here's why:

I hated the protaganist because he was
incredibly uninteresting and inept. I have never liked annoying rich boys with no visible talent, even if they pretend to have a purpose. He does not deserve anything he earns, because he does not work hard! A wealthy character certainly does not have to be vapid or stupid. If I could I would redesign this character completely. The worst part about him is that he succeeds where he should have failed! The only likeable character as one of the antagonists(Fuchs). The protaganist's transformation at the end is quite unbelievable.

Some of the events that occur are unrealistic such as the irritating love interest which manifests out of nowhere(inserted for no reason) and the whole trip is unlikely. The storyline was vaguely interesting and it was often exciting. I enjoyed the decriptions of Venus, but I'm not certain whether the book is scientifically accurate. The message about the enviroment is vague at best. I am also certain that a state of the art spaceship would cost more than 10 billion dollars. I also did not like the fact that financial experts readily agreed to lend money to fund a ridiculosuly expensive and unlikely trip to Venus.

I probably would not read this book again, however it certaintly captured my attention at the time. The books strong point is that it conjures up quite a bit of suspense and excitement. I'm not sure how this novel is comparable to other sci-fi. I'll give it a 3/5 for having a predictable ending. I had fun invisioning better endings, that would of course be more realistic.

Read this book if you want an exciting story, but if you long for character development you will leave mostly unsatisfied. If only Fuchs eccentric character styling were used on the protagainst!

SPOILER! scroll if you're planning on reading the book
I wish that the book left me with some sort of purpose, or meaning. Is the message that rich playboys will succeed because they can use their fathers' money? His motivation is truly greed, rather than concern for his brother.

Said better than I could...."

  • Nov. 11th, 2009 at 9:50 AM

The Rarity of INTJ women

"You know, I don't know that we are necessarily as, um, rare as people like to think. I think we can display differently than INTJ males, and that the INTJ description on a lot of sites leans toward the description of the INTJ male vs what an INTJ femalecan be like. For example, I wrestled with the whole INFJ thing because, hell, I dofeel; I'm not some unemotional alien. I'm married and I have two children and I love all of them and I think cats are cute and I have no desire to control the world, boo hoo ha ha. And yes, I can give good advice. And yes, I like art and literature and writing... and I HATE AYN RAND! And I think Nietzsche is a pompous windbag, although he does say a few wise things...

But I also am ridiculously in love with the classification of living things... and the Dewey Decimal System... and taking apart my kid's toys to figure out how they work...

What it boils down to, essentially, is that I LOVE to learn about things. I LOVE to learn about EVERYTHING. And I love to discuss topics, not people. I don't feel much of a desire to help people, no matter how bastardly that sounds. I mean, yeah, I want people to, uh, survive and be happy and whatever, but it's not exactly my priority, nor is it what I would consider to be my purpose or my strength. And I want my children to think on their own, to feel comfortable with being who they are. In other words, harmony is not as important to me as the assertion of individuality- not that getting along and compromise isn't important, just not as."
