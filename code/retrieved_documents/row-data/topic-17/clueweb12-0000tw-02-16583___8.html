Benefits of adopting from a humane society or shelter

  • Top Article
  • All 17 Articles

by Caryna St. John

Created on: January 07, 2009   Last Updated: October 29, 2009

When many people decide to add a four-legged best friend to their family, often the local pet shop is the first place they look. It is unfortunate that more people do not consider looking at their localanimal shelter first. There are many wonderful dogs without homes just looking for the right family.

There are a lot of myths about rescue dogs and that is why more people do not seek them out. Below, some of these myths are dispelled.


Myth #1: All rescue dogs are mutts.

The large majority of dogs that you will find at the pound are probably not purebred. There are, however, specific dog breed rescues around the country if you are looking for a particular breed. By the same token, mutts can make just as good if not better pets as purebreds.

Myth #2: There is something wrong with a rescue dog. There is a reason that the dog ended up at the pound.

The answer here is a qualified yes. Obviously, there is a reason that dogs end up at theanimal shelter, but it does not always have to do with the dog. Sometimes owners pass on before their dogs. Sometimes dogs have dysfunctional owners. Sometimes dogs just aren't the right match for a particular family but might be the right fit for you.

Myth #3: Rescue dogs are set in their ways.

It is true that if you adopt a dog, you are most likely not going to get a puppy. However, there are as many positives about this as there are negatives. If you are lucky, you will receive a dog that is already housetrained, can walk on a leash, and knows some simple commands. At the very least, you know what you are getting with an older dog.

Myth #4: Rescue dogs can't be trusted with children.

It is true that many rescue dogs end up at the pound because they couldn't be trusted around small children. It is also true, however, that there are many rescue dogs who are wonderful around children. The same is true of any dog. As long as dogs are properly matched up with owners, all will be well.

These are just some of the many reasons why rescue dogs deserve a chance at adoption. Still not sure if a rescue dog is right for you? Consider that the cost of adopting a rescue dog is often much less than buying from a reputable breeder, and the money goes toward other needy dogs. In addition, the shelter will often pay for your first vet visit, spay or neuter, and first shots. All in all, rescue dogs are a great option for many families.
