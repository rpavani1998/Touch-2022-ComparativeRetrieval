But to his 68-year-old mother, who has always had a caustic tongue, the photographs were "slightly blurred" and lacked "character". Sathyu said she should meet an eye specialist. But a month later, he decided that his mother could be right about the "monotonous" part.

After experimenting with his wife's digital camera and consulting an artist friend who suggested he buy a "proper camera", Sathyu walked into aNikon shop in Mumbai and bought a Nikon D3100, plus a few lenses, that cost around Rs 50,000. "I was also inspired by my favourite actress Priyanka Chopra [who features in a Nikon ad]," says the 35-year-old. "This time my mother didn't say the pictures were bad," he says.

The "proper camera" was a digital SLR, or single lens reflex, camera, which offers a far more accurate preview of framing compared with point-and-shoot cameras.

Renowned Mumbai-based fashion photographer Ashok Salian also swears by DSLR cameras. Only, Salian is a "loyal and exclusive" user of Canon. "Technology has made photography much easier," says Salian who has shot numerous Bollywood stars, including his favourite Dimple Kapadia before the release of Saagar in 1984. "Most people acquire a DSLR camera to stay more creative," he says.

Salian and Sathyu are part of a growing tribe of Indians, professional and amateur, who are splurging on DSLR cameras like never before, thanks to their high disposable incomes. That makes India one of the world's fastest-growing markets for such cameras, at a time when camera sales are stagnating in the West and Japan.

Capturing Moments

For Japanese arch rivals Canon and Nikon, the biggest players in the segment, India along with China is leading the charge. Between them, the two companies sell almost 99% of the DSLR cameras that Indians buy. In a country where camera penetration is a mere 6%, the growth for DSLRs has been close to 100% in the past few years, saysCanon India senior vice-president Alok Bharadwaj.

He says in the Rs 4,500-crore Indian camera market, DSLR cameras account for Rs 750 crore. Another Rs 750 crore comes from selling interchangeable lenses and accessories. One of the biggest advantages of using a DSLR is that it lets the user choose from a variety of interchangeable lenses.
