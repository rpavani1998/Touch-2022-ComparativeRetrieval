MP black or chrome, which do you prefer?

noddy B , Jun 18, 2003; 03:41 a.m.

Hi,

I am seriously considering getting an MP. I have used an M6 for about 5- 7 years before I sold it and bought D60 and a few Canon lenses. Now I miss the M feeling so much(and believe it or not, I produce more "snapshots" with the D60 and IMHO less artistic photos than with an M).

Anyway, I like both black and chrome and can't make up my mind. So I would like to ask forum members, which one do you prefer?

Cameron Sawyer , Jun 18, 2003; 05:00 a.m.

The black paint finish was invented for fondlers and is awfully impractical for a user. It will be scratched and flaked to hell very quickly if you really use the camera, just like the Voigtlander Cosina painted brass lenses.

I like black cameras in general because they are less visible and more discreet, but if I were you I would pass on the black paint MP. The chrome finish should be quite bulletproof which will allow you to use the camera without being obsessed with not letting it touch anything.

noddy B , Jun 18, 2003; 05:20 a.m.

Can anyone with an old black M body whose MP-like paint has severely worn off post the picture of his/her camera?

After a few years of use my M6 had scratches at the bottom and black colour peeled off around the corners. That kind of worn-off does not bother me. But from reading some previous posts (yes, I have done the search) it seems that a black MP can be much worse.

Laurent Berta , Jun 18, 2003; 05:21 a.m.

I prefer black

Steve Barnett , Jun 18, 2003; 06:10 a.m.

The black paint on the MP is much tougher than the old paint used on M3 and M2 cameras. It does not peel off. The idea that it is for 'fondlers' only comes from people who worry about resale value, and can't imagine a camera that shows any signs of use. In fact, thinking about it, who is the 'fondler'? Somebody who uses a black paint MP even if the edges are going to show brass after a while, or is it somebody who worries about maintaining the showroom finish of his or her cameras? In twenty years time I am sure you would like the finish. And at least it can be re-painted if you don't like it! But the chrome is a more durable finish without doubt.
