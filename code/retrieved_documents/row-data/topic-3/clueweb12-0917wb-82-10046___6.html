Nikon D300 DX Review

Posted by Olivia Salis March 10, 2012 No Comments

Nikon D300 DX was announced as one of the best Nikon DSLR camera. It was actually included on the top five best Nikon camera options over $900. Nikon D300 DX has been made with quality features and performance that every photographer will surely enjoy. Nikon D300 DX’s 12.3-efficient-megapixel D300 is a combination of brand new and advanced technology from D3 that gives them an edge compared to other brands of DSLR cameras. Just like D3, Nikon D300 DX features the EXPEED Image Processing System that increases the camera’s speed and processing power. In addition, Nikon D300 DX has a new 51-point autofocus system, 3D Focus Tracking feature, and two new LiveView shooting modes that can frame a photo through the use of Nikon D300 DX’s high-resolution LCD monito. Nikon D300 DX also has the same Scene Recognition System as with D3 that improves the accuracy of autofocus, autoexposure, and auto white balance. Nikon D300 DX responds with lightning speed, powering up in a sheer 0.13 seconds and shooting with an unnoticeable 45-millisecond shutter release interval time. Nikon D300 DX can also shoot at six frames per second but if you are going to use the MB-D10 multi-power battery pack, it shoot as fast as eight frames per second.Check out Nikon D300 DX’s availability now by clicking here.

Another feature of Canon D300 DX is the new intelligent array of 15 cross-type sensors and 36 horizontal sensors. The sensors can be used used individually or even in groups. In addition, Nikon has a new Picture Control System which makes it easier to adjust the settings making it more convenient for new Nikon D300 DX users. Actually, there are four

basic setting options such as Neutral, Vivid, Standard, and Monochrome. which can be modified easily to adjust and customize the image’s sharpness, tone compensation, brightness, and saturation. Nikon D300 has a new Active D-Lighting mode that gives an amazing real-time highlight and shadow improvement with optimized image contrast.to enhance the photo’s appearance and avoid doing too much edits.You can either choose from Tripod mode or Handheld mode for the LiveView. to get the focus and to capture more accurate photos. You do not have to worry about dusts that might enter your camera because Nikon D300 DX has a new self-cleaning sensor unit to reduce the accumulation of dusts.

If you are going to buy your Nikon D300 DX at Amazon, the box will be composed of Nikon D300 DX digital SLR body, one EN-EL3e rechargeable li-ion battery, MH-18a quick charger, UC-E4 USB cable, EG-D100 video cable, AN-D300 strap, BM-8 LCD monitor cover, body cap, DK-5 eyepiece cap, DK-23 rubber eyecup and Software Suite CD-ROM.

Here is the summary for Nikon D300′s features and specifications:

-12.3-megapixel

- 3.0-inch LiveView LCD display

- shoots up to 100 shots at full 12.3-megapixel resolution

- EXPEED Image Processing System

- self-cleaning sensor unit

As a proof that Nikon D300 DX is really an amazing DSLR camera, read this review:

 

I have used this camera for only a few days and am very satisfied and enthusiastic. Nikon has made an excellent product. You can change the camera’s ISO very quickly and it goes up to 3200. Very useful.
There are many playback features such as zooming in close on an image and navigating around in it. It is a big improvement over previous models. The new playback screen is much bigger and has an awesome resolution. You can live preview on it. Very cool!There are several focus options, including the 51 point focus. You can press help on any menu item and it will explain what that item is clearly and easy to read.
The menu is easy to navigate and easy to use. The camera is comfortable to hold, has a solid feel and all of the controls are within easy reach. Shooting mode, ISO and most other controls can be easily changed without going into the menu. All the above are features, but the most important benefit is excellent and sharp images.

- Walter O. Koenig “Amoxtli”from San Diego, California

Here are the following pros and cons of Nikon D300 DX:

Pros:

- it has a fantastic bright viewfinder

- great auto-focus

- bright 3.0 inch LCD

-  has a useful in-camera picture editing

- features a useful LiveView

- excellent high ISO performance

- menu is easy to navigate

Cons:

- the arrangement of controls is quite confusing

- quite expensive

Conclusion:

Nikon has made a great improvement for their Nikon D300 DX. Although the price is quite expensive, it’s all worth it.
