          I've heard that the Canon kit lens is actually pretty good. (I don't know; I'm a Nikon guy.)

          But a lot of the newer lenses don't "feel good" to old-timers since they're lighter-weight and
          have less friction in focusing so that auto-focus can work faster. I know that in the old days
          of pure manual-focus lenses, it was nice to have friction so you could smoothly make small
          adjustments.

          So all modern lenses may "feel funny". But the key thing is how you like the camera and
          its controls, et cetera. It could be that you like the Pentax because its controls are similar
          to what you're used to on your old Pentax, and that's a very good reason to stick with the
          brand. join to post

                Re: Didn't like the way Nikon D50 and Canon rebel/20 D felt

                Thu, June 15, 2006 - 4:31 PM
                Yes, both Canon 50mm's, the f/1.8 and the f/1.4 are very nice optically, and they're reasonably tough. They're really nice on a 20D, because they're effectively 80mm lens, which is pretty nice working distance and it's also for a wicked fast lens. Great for interiors, club, and stage lighting situations.

                In general, I find the L-series lenses focus FASTER than the 20D kit lens, despite any disparities in mass or friction. However, that 20D kit lens is really not terrible optically and it is light. This zoom demonstrated to me that Canon was serious about supporting the 1.6X FOV form factor cameras via the EF-S lens line, where the image circle is set back farther toward the sensor into the camera. I think it may even be a sort of "second-generation" design, because if you look carefully at the 2X Teleconvertor II (say, for use with the 70-200mm f/2.8L IS), you see a very similar optical interfacing trick.

                I keep saying "20D," but the EF-S mount of course applies to the new 30D and the RebelXT as well. join to post
