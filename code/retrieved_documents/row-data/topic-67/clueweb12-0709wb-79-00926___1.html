No TOEFL Required for ASU Admission

If you successfully complete the AECP Advanced 2 Level, you can receive a TOEFL waiver for admission to Arizona State University unless the degree program has a special or higher requirement. In addition to completing AECP, for ASU undergraduate or graduate admission you must also fulfill all other ASU admission requirements.

For F-1 visa students, all Advanced 2 classes must be satisfactorily completed within a single AECP session (or within a single ASU semester if doing AECP/ASU concurrent enrollment). A TOEFL waiver from AECP is valid for 2 years from the completion date, exactly like a TOEFL score. For more information, please see an AECP Student Advisor.

 

ASU Undergraduate English Language Requirements for a TOEFL Waiver

If you complete AECP's Advanced 2 Level with grades of “A” or “B” in all core and elective classes, you do not need a TOEFL score for admission to most undergraduate programs at Arizona State University. The ASU Undergraduate Catalog states: “Applicants who have completed the Advanced 2 Level of the American English and Culture Program are exempt from the English proficiency requirement.”

  • Undergraduate Admissions will not accept AECP Advanced 2 core class grades lower than “B.” SeeASU International Undergraduate Admissions Policies.
  • See AECP's Elective Courses page for a list of courses highly recommended for students who wish to enroll as an ASU undergraduate (recommended courses are marked with  .)
  • After successfully completing the AECP Advanced 2 Level, you may request an official AECP completion letter addressed to ASU Undergraduate Admissions.
     
ASU Graduate English Language Requirements for a TOEFL Waiver

If you complete the AECP's Advanced 2 Level with grades of “A” or “B” in core andrequired elective classes, you do not need a TOEFL score for admission to most graduate programs at Arizona State University. The ASU Graduate Catalog states: “Applicants who have completed the American English and Culture Program Advanced 2 Level are exempt from the English proficiency requirement.”

  • You must select your Advanced 2 elective classes from the courses marked with on the AECP Elective Courses page.
  • Graduate Admissions will not accept any Advanced 2 grades lower than “B.” SeeASU International Graduate Admissions Policies.
  • To receive a TOEFL waiver for admission to the Sandra Day O’Connor College of Law MLS program, you must have grades of “A” in all Advanced 2 classes.
  • After successfully completing the AECP Advanced 2 Level, you may request an official AECP completion letter addressed to ASU Graduate Admissions.

 

TOEFL Preparation at AECP

 The TOEFL test (Test Of English as a Foreign Language) measures your ability to use and understand the English language as it is read, written, heard and spoken in the university classroom. As the most accepted English-language test in the world.Scores from this exam can be used by corporations, governments, and other organizations to assess your English language proficiency.

  • AECP offers intermediate and advanced TOEFL Preparation courses.
  • AECP has full-time student advisors to help students with admission to ASU.

 

Taking the TOEFL

ASU University Testing and Scanning Services (UTS) offers the ASU Institutional TOEFL. For test dates and information, and for registration details, visit theASU Institutional TOEFL webpage.

The ASU Institutional TOEFL is offered only to current AECP and ASU students, and the test score can only be used at ASU, not any other institutions.

Early registration is encouraged. Test seats are limited and fill up quickly.

To learn more about taking the TOEFL at other locations, visit the official TOEFL website.

 

IELTS & Other English Language Proficiency Tests

Other English language proficiency tests that are accepted for ASU admission include:

 

  • iBT (internet-based TOEFL): The iBT is given at locations in Tempe and Phoenix. Location details are available when you register online. Website:http://www.ets.org/toefl/ibt
  • PTE Academic (Pearson):  There is a testing center in Phoenix. Directions are available when you register. Website: http://pearsonpte.com/PTEAcademic.
  • IELTS: Currently, the nearest test location is in Tucson, Arizona. Tucson is approximately than 2 hours away by car from ASU. Website: http://www.ielts.org (on the website, click “Test Takers” for details.)

More contact information is available on the UTS website. http://www.asu.edu/uts/e_institutional_toefl.htm
