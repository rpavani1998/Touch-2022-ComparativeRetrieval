New Yahoo’s Search Clues vs Google Trends

Tweet

Yahoo! have launched a new tool for to spot search trends called Yahoo! Search Clues. While Yahoo clues is kind of similar to Google trends, there are a few differences also. Let’s compare Yahoo clues with Google trends.

Which is best: Yahoo Search Clues or Google Trends?

1. Graphic Appearance

Which of the two keyword trend tools are most graphically appealing?

Google Trends
Google trends look quite boring to be honest. It’s very basic, probably to optimise site speed which is focus for Google. On the positive side with the ‘simple graphics’ is that the page loads very quick, use Google data and show news reference volume below the search volume trend.

Yahoo! Search Clues
There is no doubt that Yahoo clues looks way better than Google trends. Yahoo’s search chart is both clearer and more attractive. You are quickly able to get more info about the target audience, where women are displayed in pink and men in blue.
A nice map is also included, displaying where people using Yahoo! comes from.

Grades: 1-0 to Yahoo! Search Clues.

2. Functionality

What is the difference in functionality between Yahoo! Search Clues and Google Trends?

Google trends
+Positive with .csv export, so data can be used after leaving the Google trend website.
+Google trends are helpful to find out news that might be possible reasons for traffic volume changes.

-Don’t show demographic data, such as age and gender.
-No related keywords are displayed.

Yahoo Clues
+Gender: Seems to be useful advice.
+Age: Very helpful information for advertisers and when ad texts and slogans are written.
+Related searches: Both previous and next queries are displayed. However, I find it very unlikely that a majority of people searching for search engine optimization type ‘search engine’ or anything related to ‘dogpile’ after. That simply makes no sense at all.

-Cannot export data to excel or PDF, which limit usage quite a bit.
-Yahoo doesn’t show any reasons for why search volume might increase or decrease over time.

Grades: 2-1 to Yahoo! clues

3. Data reliability

Which data is most reliable, Google’s or Yahoo’s?

Google: Keywords and press releases
I’m confident that press releases are one of the major factors for increasing search volume. But what about negative factors influencing search volume? Perhaps Google trends could show bank holidays and other reasons to fluctuating search volume as well.

Yahoo: Gender and age
SEO Specialist believe data for both gender and age can be very useful for media buyers, media agencies and search engine optimisers. It’s likely Yahoo use data from Yahoo mail to draw conclusions about gender and age.

Google: Charts displayed
Google trends offers charts and data for a wide range of keywords. It’s very convenient to use to spot trend changes.

Yahoo: Charts displayed
Only two keyword out of 20 I tried was shown in Yahoo search clues. I wish they had this service for more keywords. The biggest issue for Yahoo is probably that very few people search in Yahoo for terms related to search engine optimization.

Grades: 2-2 between Google trends and Yahoo search clues.
