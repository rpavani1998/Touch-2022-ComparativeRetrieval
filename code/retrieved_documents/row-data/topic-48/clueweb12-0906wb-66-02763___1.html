    Just say No to Extra Sauce! (And Other Tips to Make Family Pizza Night Healthy)

    By Jessica Reinhart

    So you're geared up for your weekly family pizza night, money saving coupon in hand, and stop to wonder – should we really be ordering pizza? Can all that crust, cheese and pepperoni be good for my family? While it's a far cry from a healthy food choice, there are ways to make this American staple healthier. We've put together some quick ordering tips that will satisfy your families' cravings and keep them on a healthy track.

    Get your greens. It may seem like a no-brainer, but ordering a healthful garden salad to accompany a less than healthy pizza will ensure you get your daily dose of greens and help you feel fuller faster – thus limiting the urge to overindulge on pie.

    Go thin. Opt for thin crust pizza instead of regular and save a whopping 90 calories per slice!

    Keep it whole. Ordering heart healthy whole wheat crust instead of regular crust will boost your fiber intake by 50 percent.

    "Just say NO!"to extra sauce. Although tomato sauce is high in lycopene, a powerful antioxidant, many pizza restaurants include high fructose corn syrup (HFCS) amongst their sauces' secret ingredients. Check your favorite haunt's Web site for a list of ingredients to see if it's included – if the answer's yes, ask for light sauce, or opt for a white pizza instead.

    Meat lovers beware. Meats like pepperoni, bacon, meatballs and sausage can add anywhere from 10 to 40 calories and 1.5 to 3.5 grams of fat per slice. If meat is a must, substitute for healthier alternatives, such as chicken or shrimp.

    Go heavy on the veggies. Veggies are not only nutritious, but also low in calories, which gives you the green light to pile them on. We love sun-dried tomatoes, onions, broccoli, spinach, olives, spinach, peppers, mushrooms and artichoke hearts.
