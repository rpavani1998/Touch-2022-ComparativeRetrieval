Recipe: Pizza Pasta Soup

Issue: February 2012, Posted Date: 2/13/2012
Tweet

 


A warm bowl of Pasta Pizza Soup hits the spot on a cold day. It’s a great meal when you’re trying to lose a few pounds.

The soup is low in sodium and high in flavor. If you’re concerned about sodium, skip the Parmesan cheese and you’ll have only 120 milligrams in a serving.

                            Enjoy,
                            Bonnie


Bonnie Brost is a registered dietitian with the Wellness Program at Essentia Health’s Heart & Vascular Center. She’s also a certified diabetes educator. Bonnie’s goal is to show people that eating healthy can be easy and tasty.  


Pasta Pizza Soup

1 tablespoon olive oil
1 pound super lean ground beef (90 percent lean)
8 ounces fresh mushrooms, sliced
1 medium onion, chopped
1 celery stalk, thinly sliced or chopped
½ cup red or yellow bell peppers, chopped
1 garlic clove, minced
2 cups water
14½-ounce can no-salt-added diced tomatoes
2 medium carrots, shredded
4 cups no-salt-added beef broth (sodium less than 200 mg per cup)
1½ teaspoons dried oregano or Chef Paul Prudhommes’s Herbal Pasta & Pizza Seasoning
½ teaspoon turmeric (optional)
½ teaspoon pepper
2 cups cooked whole wheat pasta (4 ounces dry)
12 tablespoons Parmesan cheese

Heat olive oil in a large stockpot over medium heat. Add beef, mushrooms, onion, celery, bell peppers and garlic. Cook until meat is no longer pink. Stir in water, tomatoes, carrots, broth and seasonings. Bring to a boil. Reduce heat; cover and simmer for 45-60 minutes or until carrots are tender. Stir in cooked pasta; heat through. Serve with 1 tablespoon of parmesan cheese.

Nutrition Facts
 Servings: 12
Size: 1 cup
Calories: 130
Total Fat: 6.5 g
Saturated Fat: 2.5 g
Trans Fat: 0 Cholesterol: 30 mg
Sodium: 200 mg
Carbohydrates: 12 g
Fiber: 2.5 g
Protein : 13 g
