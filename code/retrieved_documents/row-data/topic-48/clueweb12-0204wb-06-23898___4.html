Pasta Nutrition Power

Food Fact

By Lisa Mosing, MS, RD, FADA, Special to LifeScript Published September 20, 2004

Pasta is truly a global food. Just about every country has a form of pasta, from Asian noodles to spaetzele. Moreover, archaeologists now believe that noodles probably came from Asia as early as 1000 B.C, although it can be found today in some form or other, in North America, Europe, South America, and Asia. Today, pasta is out of favor with some people following a low carbohydrate diet. However, most health professionals consider pasta, especially whole-wheat pasta, an excellent source of complex carbohydrates.

Contrary to popular opinion, pasta does provide nutrients, such as iron, niacin and B vitamins. A serving of pasta is roughly 1 cup of cooked (2 ounces, uncooked pasta) which has about 200 calories. It can be found at the base of the USDA Food Guide Pyramid. And, until your family begins to enjoy pasta made with whole wheat or some other whole-grain flour, try to mix half regular with half of the traditional pasta noodles. Adding whole-grain pasta will increase the fiber intake of everyone. Although pasta appears healthier when spinach, tomatoes or other vegetables are added to plain pasta dough, the amount consumed for each serving provides little more than a coloring since the pasta will only contain about one teaspoon of vegetables. AddingPersonalized Supplements is always a good idea to make sure you are not missing any nutrients. What each family mixes with pasta is as unique as pizza toppings. The "American Pasta Report" found that that almost 40 % of people prefer cheese on top of pasta, and it is not surprising that the choice of vegetables and meat was comparable.

 
Perfect Pasta
Pasta is almost fail proof to make a quick meal. Just add vegetables and lean protein for a healthy meal. Since most people tend to overestimate portion sizes, remember that a serving of pasta, any form, is 1 cup cooked or 2 ounces dried. For a family, plan on 8 ounces of small to medium shaped pasta, such as elbow macaroni, spaghetti, fettuccine making 4 cups of cooked pasta. Leftover pasta should be refrigerated in an airtight container about 4 days. A dash of vegetable oil can keep the leftover cooked pasta from sticking. For a taste change from sandwiches at lunch, take leftover cooked pasta, add tuna or cooked beans, chopped vegetables, and reduced fat Italian dressing. Refrigerate cooked pasta in an airtight container for 3 to 5 days.

Whether fresh or dried pasta is preferred by your family, most supermarkets carry both varieties. However, both require different cooking and storage rules. Today many people prefer dried pasta and a box or two stashed in the kitchen pantry. Fresh pasta will as a rule, cook faster than dried versions.

Many people are confused about how much water to use for each pound of pasta. Culinary experts recommend that you use a large pan that will hold about 4-6 quarts of water for each pound of pasta. The box will tell you how long to boil the water. Then drain; never rinse the pasta with cold water unless the noodles will be used in a cold dish, like a pasta salad. Rate This Article
