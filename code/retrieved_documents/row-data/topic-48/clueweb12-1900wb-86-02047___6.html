Spring Time Recipe: Peace, Love and Happiness Pasta in a Lemon Shallot Sauce

Thursday, April 26th, 2012

A great Spring time recipe is our New Peace, Love and Happiness Pasta in a delicious Lemon Shallot Sauce. This is a great, vegetarian recipe that is as earthy and delicious as it sounds. It pairs so well with the shapes in this pasta- making for a great dinner or side dish. Loaded with vegetables it is healthy and delicious both hot and cold! The recipe comes on the back of this pasta or just follow the instructions below to enjoy!

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Step 1: The Ingredients!

14 oz. Bag of Peace, Love and Happiness Pasta, ½ cup oil packed sundried tomatoes, drained and chopped, 14 oz. can quartered artichoke hearts, drained, 3 oz. (about 3 cups packed) fresh spinach leaves, cut or torn, ¾ cup minced shallots (about 4 medium sized shallots), 1 T. olive oil, ½ cup lemon juice (fresh squeezed- Use less if you don’t love lemons), 2 cups vegetable stock, divided, 1 cup dry white wine or apple juice, 1 T. cornstarch, ¼ cup chopped parsley 1 t. (or more) dried thyme or 1 T. fresh thyme, Zest of 2 lemons (or 1 if you want more of a hint of lemon), Parmesan cheese for topping and Salt and Pepper to taste.

Step 2: In a large skillet or wok, saute shallots in olive oil over medium heat for about 4 minutes or until translucent.

Step 3: Stir in lemon juice, 1 cup vegetable stock, and wine. Bring to a low boil and cook for about 10 minutes. While it is cooking start boiling the water for the pasta.

Step 4: In a small bowl, whisk cornstarch into remaining 1 cup stock until smooth. Slowly pour in to shallot mixture, stirring constantly and cook until sauce is thickened and smooth, about 2 minutes. Stir in parsley, thyme, zest, and salt and pepper to taste.

Step 5: Add sundried tomatoes, artichoke quarters, and fresh spinach and stir until spinach wilts. Keep sauce warm.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Step 6: Cook pasta in gently boiling water for approx. 6 minutes until tender but firm (salt optional).

Step 7: Drain pasta and combine with sauce. Top with Parmesan cheese!

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Bon Appetit!

-Megan Grehan

The Truth About Carbs- An Objective View From the Fun Pasta People Themselves

Thursday, August 11th, 2011

  Well, isn’t this the question of the ages. In recent years, our friend the carb has been made out to be the bad guy….every time. Sugar, flour, potatoes….even fruits have been banned in many of these “miracle” diets. We’ve all heard of, and probably tried, the Atkins diet. I tried it. It lasted for a complete day, and I was quite proud of myself. But, it made me feel terrible and truly was not a pleasant experience.

         So… what really is a carb, and why do we love (and need) them? A carb, full name carbohydrate, is a component of food used for ENERGY in the body. That’s right, you don’t get your energy from that bun less loaded bacon cheeseburger Atkins encouraged you to gorge on, you get more from the fries.

Hmmm probably not…..

        Last time I checked, you need energy to work out.  Our bodies crave pastas, breads and rice because we need them for our bodies to function properly. You may have heard of net carbs, which are the kind with fiber and lower sugar, like PASTA AND WHOLE WHEAT BREADS!!! These are the kinds to fill up on! Nix the fries and go for a potato. Pass up the huge bowl of spaghetti or deep dish pizza and go for some whole wheat pasta dishes. Even regular white pastas contain a plentiful amount of the “good carbs”.

      Not to mention, our pasta is a lot healthier than many other fattening and sugar loaded fundraising options. Which is why we reccomend pairing any of our pastas with a zesty marinara or olive oil sauce component and …voila! Not only are you getting your energy and fiber, your piling on those vitamins, antioxidants and omega 3’s. Not to mention all of our pasta has 6 grams or more of protein per serving, and very low sugar! And for those of you who take extra care, check out our Petite Pasta Nests….pretty nifty little things with 100 calories a nest. They come in whole wheat, spinach basil garlic, and tri-color. They really are delicious, and full of the good-for-you carbs.

Mmmmm Probably yes……….   

So are carbs really bad for you? Absolutely not. Just like everything else in life, balance is the key.

-By Anna Morris
