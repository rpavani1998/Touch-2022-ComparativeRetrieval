Curriculum: Online Courses: Making Music In Cyberspace FAQ's

 

Frequently Asked Questions about Farmington Public School’s Music in Cyberspace On-Line Course

What is an online course?

Online courses, correspondence courses and distance learning are all terms for a similar idea. Students enrolled in any of these are in the process of undertaking educational classes or programs remotely from the schools, campuses or physical institutions that provide them. Students enrolled in online courses should have access to a computer, Internet connection, and an email account. Students are expected to complete assignments and lessons they receive through communications with an instructor. The delivery of instruction, assignments, tests etc. is usually provided through some type of a course management system.

Judy Nichols from Oakland Schools described Online learning best, “It’s mediated communication for the purpose of instruction and learning across space and time” (personal communication, August 28, 2008).

 

How does an online course fit in my daily schedule?

This online course is taken outside of your daily high school course schedule. It is in addition your face to face classes and is a way to earn extra credit, possibly raise your grade point average, and experience online instruction. Many full and part time college students enroll in online courses. Experiencing an online course helps you prepare for higher education. As an online course it is an addition to your schedule but credits will be accumulated toward possible early graduation.

 

What are Course Management Systems?

Course management systems are usually named by the developer. You may have heard of Blackboard, Angel, or Moodle. Schools in the Oakland Schools Consortium (of which Farmington Public School is a part) use Moodle Course Management System. Course management systems, among other things, allow students and instructors to share ideas, share lessons, blog, create wikis, upload assignments, upload quizzes and tests, and chat in synchronous or asynchronous sessions.  all in a closed, password protected environments. A password is required to enter the Moodle website and all grades are locked and available only to course administrator access.

 

What is Moodle?

Moodle is a course management system. Students receive a password and access to a Moodle site that is open for them to use for the duration of the semester that they are enrolled. Moodle has many functions and components. Think of Moodle as a physical classroom where students are assigned to meet to gather information, discuss, and receive assignments. The only difference is that this classroom is in cyberspace. Moodle participants can access the site at any time and anywhere as long as they have a computer and internet access.

 

What’s so great about online instruction?

Online instruction provides you with an opportunity to take a course you may not have had time in your schedule for. Online courses allow you to earn extra credit and possibly raise your grade point average. Experiencing online instruction is a terrific way to prepare for higher education. Many full and part time college students enroll in online courses. Students who take online courses find that they must be self-motivated and take initiative about their own learning. These are important traits for successful students, no matter what grade.

 

What is Music in Cyberspace?

Music in Cyberspace  is an on-line music technology course offered by the Farmington Public School District. This high school course is offered for .5 fine arts credit. This course satisfies Michigan High School Michigan Merit Curriculum .5 credit, Performing and Applied Arts requirement as well as the Online Learning Graduation Requirements.           

 

Is there a fee?

This course is free to in-district students. A similar course offered outside of the district would be several hundred dollars. This is the first semester it is offered and we are anticipating that it will be a popular course. 

 

What are the course objectives?

Students enrolled in the course will utilize music technology in arranging, composing, and performing. This course is for anyone interested in becoming a better musician using cutting edge technology. Music experience from beginners to advanced may enroll.

 

What do I need to do to enroll?

Check out the enrollment link on the webpage. Download the documents, acquire the needed signatures and return the forms to Dr. Sheri E. Jaffurs, 30175 Highmeadow Rd., Farmington, Mi 48335. If you are able to scan documents, you may scan them and email to sheri.jaffurs@farmington.k12.mi.us

 

How often is the course offered?

The course is offered in the fall and spring, twice a year. Enrollment is limited.  

How long is the course?

The course runs for 12 weeks.

 

Who do I contact if I need more information?

Sheri.jaffurs@farmington.k12.mi.us
