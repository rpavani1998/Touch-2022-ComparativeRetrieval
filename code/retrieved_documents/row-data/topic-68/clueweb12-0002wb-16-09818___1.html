Collaboration in #physicaltherapy Creates BETTER Physical Therapists

Disappointed... very disappointed is how I currently feel.

You're all familiar with the Staples commercial and the "Easy" button, right? I tend to think we all prefer life to be easy. Is there any reason we sometimes choose to complicate matters?

Any successful company always, always researches what is out there in the world before even beginning to create a new product or offer a new service. Generally speaking, companies are "for profit" and of course, they have to calculate their return on investment in any endeavor.

Individual physical therapists and our associations most certainly are not "for profit." Even though that is the case, individual physical therapists and our association are no different. A researcher has an idea... the first step is always a literature search to learn what is known in the field about the idea. I blog frequently enough... the first step is always knowing what's out there before I post so I have a unique perspective to bring to all of you. Hopefully, we all continually learn and grow in our areas of interest. I assume you each have found informal options where you learn - whether it is Twitter or blogs or forums or something even larger like Physiopedia. As we each become experts and continue to grow, did you happen to see something very common in all of those informal options? An expert never becomes an expert independently. An expert becomes an expert via engagement, discussion and communication. Do you know why that is? Experts are curious individuals. No one becomes an expert in a lone environment.

Last night, I saw a tweet from @MPascoe

Just saw demo of http://t.co/3o8XQJP - reminds me and @SPascoeDPT of @physiopedia - hmmm.... #APTA

I got curious and had to click... and then, I was more than disappointed. Our American Physical Therapy Association didn't do its homework. Sadly, the first step was forgotten. Before rolling out some supposed "new" idea - the research aspect. Our membership dollars were wasted on trying to reinvent the wheel. From what I read on PT Now, the product won't be as good as what is already out there. One stop shopping where those contributing have to be nominated? Come on... that is so... so.. top-downish!

I vote the APTA take my membership dollars that are going to be spent on PT Now and give that money to Rachael Lowe. Lowe has given herself freely to Physiopedia. Yes, she chose to do this and yes, it started as "her baby." It's a great free product for anyone with internet access. It is a project bigger than herself. It's an international project. It is a single stopping point of information which allows contribution from any physical therapist. I truly don't understand why the APTA wouldn't want to immediately jump on board with Physiopedia. I would like to think the APTA not only wants us Americans to be "evidence based," but would also like to see Canadians and Scottish and British and Dutch and Australian and Mexican and Italian and African and... ALL have an option to freely contribute and learn.

I don't want my membership dollars going toward a selfish project... I don't want my membership dollars going toward some spin-off product that won't be as good... and I don't want my membership dollars going toward something that discludes so many by elevating others to contribute because they were nominated and have "experience"... I don't want my membership dollars going toward a stagnant, filtered product. I want to say NO to PT Now and Yes to Physiopedia. I want to say yes to helping the whole profession - members or not members of our association, United States citizens or not... in this situation, I want my association to choose to collaborate and help all.

So... how many of you agree with my perspective?

~Selena

Oh... just a little disclaimer... of course, this is just my opinion
