Everybody uses Internet Explorer to surf the 'net, right? Well, not quite any more. Our web stats do show that IE is the overwhelming choice of our readers, though there is a growing trend towards the "Firefox" browser.

If you haven't tried the "Firefox" browser from Mozilla.org, you're missing out on some great features missing from IE. (not to mention better security from all of those hackers going after flaws in IE). Some say once Microsoft got market dominance, they stopped working on improvements to IE. It's a free download at:
http://www.mozilla.org/products/firefox/

Here is a tech article that gives you 13 reasons why FIREFOX is better than Internet Explorer.
http://www.flexbeta.net/main/articles.php?action=show&id=32

We switched a while back and haven't looked back. No, they are not a website sponsor, just a good product. And it's free!

Some "hot key" shortcuts for you Firefox browser

Quote:
ALT-D
to jump insertion point to address bar.

Instead of lunging for your mouse, try this slick technique for opening a new page. Press ALT-D, and your insertion point (cursor) will jump to the address bar. Then simply overtype the existing address.

CTRL-Enter
to type "www...com" quickly.

When combined with ALT-D above, this works wonders! Once you are in the address bar, there is no need to type thehttp:// prefix or the www. or the .com domain names. Simply type the middle portion of the desired address, and press CTRL-Enter.

e.g. If you type CNN and press CTRL-Enter, you will go tohttp://www.cnn.com

CTRL-Mouse wheel spin
to enlarge or shrink page font size

That screen font is too small or too large for you? No worries, simply spin your mousewheel while holding CTRL. Your font size changes incrementally.

CTRL-T
to launch a new tab

A favorite trick for power Internet users... this enables you to read your Gmail or Hotmail, while searching Google, and simultaneously checking your stocks and weather reports.

CTRL-T launches a new tab (effectively a new browser window, but without all the memory overhead of a full window).

SHIFT-Click a link

to launch a new window with the link.

If you are doing research, and wish to keep your current location while browsing another, use SHIFT while you click on a link. The new page opens in a second window.

If you are using the Firefox browser, you may already know about "extensions", those slick little add ons for your browser that make life easier. Now that the new and improved Firefox version 1.5 has been out for a week or two, many of the "extensions" have been updated for the new version. We won't begin to try to cover all the goodies, except to say: "Click Tools, Extensions, and Get More extensions". You can peruse the pages at Mozilla to see what is there.

We will take time to plug one that many of you will find handy. It'sFoxmarks, which permits you to sych your bookmarks, so that if you use firefox on more than one computer (work and home, or home and laptop), you can one-click your bookmarks for both.

Here's the link to download it:
http://www.foxcloud.com/wiki/Main_Page

You'll see a note that they are in "Beta", but I can tell you from first hand experience that it works great.

On a related note, remember that you can drag and drop your favorite sites onto the Firefox browser bar right below the URL. So you can have sites like Fastpitchwest, GHFL.ca, ISCfastpitch, Mornng Brief, The Deuce and more all right there on your browser for easy access.
