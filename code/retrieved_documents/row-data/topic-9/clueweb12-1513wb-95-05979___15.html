Put Your Computer on a USB Key and Live CD/DVD

Even better than that, if you have a large capacity USB key and a live CD, you can carry the very essence of your computer anywhere you go with just with a USB key and a CD. Better yet, use a live DVD.

We discuss this more fully in our article Give the Gift of Knoppix Linux and a Book for Less Than $30 -- Knoppix for Dummies.

In essence, a live CD or DVD is one to which an operating system such as GNU-Linux or Microsoft Windows has been installed. The live CD or DVD is bootable. Thus, when you boot the computer in which you have placed a live CD or DVD, it boots into the operating system installed on the live CD or DVD -- not into the operating system installed on the computer.

That let's you run your live Linux CD/DVD and software on any up-to-date PC, anywhere, anytime -- regardless of which operating system is installed on the PC. You can think of your Linux live CD/DVD as a portable computer on a disc and USB key -- and for only whatever you paid for the USB key.

There are several ways you can obtain a live Linux CD or DVD. For example you can download one from either the Knoppix of Mepis Web sites. Or you can buy a book that includes a live Linux CD or DVD such asKnoppix For Dummies (by Paul G. Sery, Wiley, ISBN: 0-7645-9779-5. $29.99),Knoppix Hacks - 100 Industrial-Strength Tips & Tools, (By Kyle Rankin, O'Reilly, ISBN: 0-596-00787-6. $30),Point & Click Linux!, (By Robin Miller, Pearson Prentice Hall, ISBN: 0131488724. $30). (Links in theResources section at the end of this article.)

These books make very nice gifts too. The DVD that comes with Knoppix For Dummies is current, which perhaps makes it the best choice for a gift. The CDs that come withKnoppix Hacks - 100 Industrial-Strength Tips & Tools and Point & Click Linux! are outdated so you, or to whomever you give those books, will need to download the current versions of Knoppix or Mepis.

Knoppix For Dummies and Point & Click Linux! are better choices for new computer users andnew Linux users. Knoppix Hacks - 100 Industrial-Strength Tips & Tools likely is a better choice for the more experienced computer user. In addition to the Mepis Linux live CD,Point & Click Linux! also includes a DVD with video tutorials about using Mepis Linux and the live CD.

Incidentally, Robin Miller also has a book similar to his Point & Click Linux! book, Point & Click OpenOffice.org! The OpenOffice book also has a DVD with video tutorials. Please seeOffice on the Linux Desktop in the sidebar on the right for our coverage of OpenOffice. OpenOffice isfree and open source software (FOSS) that is comparable to Microsoft Office and is compatible with the Linux, Mac, and Windows operating systems.

The Pentax Optio S6 Digital Camera

It's amazing but really not so amazing how popular digital photography has become over the past few years. One reason it is not so amazing is that while prices of digital cameras have come way down over the years, the quality of the cameras and the photographs they take has gone way up.

We have a review of the Pentax Optio S6 digital camera (less than $350) in the works. So far we like it very much. It sports a six megapixels resolution and 3X optical zoom in a package that is smaller and lighter than a pack of cigarettes.

The ISO range on the Pentax Optio S6 is from 80 to 400. That means it can take good pictures from very dim to very bright lighting conditions. Moreover, the S6 can automatically adjust the ISO setting in order to optimize the lighting quality of your pictures.

The Pentax Optio S6 is chock full of great and important photographic features. And we believe thePentax Optio S6 digital camera makes a great gift for the people at the top of your gift list -- and for you, too.
