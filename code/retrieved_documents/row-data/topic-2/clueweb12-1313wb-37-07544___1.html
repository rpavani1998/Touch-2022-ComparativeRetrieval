Desktop or Laptop for Online College

Have you ever wondered which computer works best for you, a desktop or laptop foronline college? Well, wonder no more. It really comes down to personal preference and budgetary constraints. In reality, a computer exists for every person and every budget.

Desktops

Which is right for you, a desktop or laptop for online college? Each type of computer offers a specific set of advantages and disadvantages for each potential owner. You must evaluate which is right for you. Many times, a desktop computer provides users with a greater value in overall product offerings for the price you will pay. As laptops grew in popularity, the price of desktops lowered. Not everyone needs a laptop. Many people work from home.  Therefore, they take their courses through online universities from home. Having a desktop provides people with more storage, memory, and faster speed than a typical laptop provides for the average student.

If you cannot afford a computer in your home, the public library offers free use of their desktop computers. If you have a two-hour exam and the library only allows for one-hour usage periods, speak with someone who can help you extend your computer time. Most libraries will allow exceptions, if you speak with them beforehand. If you have a desktop in your home and it is quite old, you can upgrade your system without incurring a huge investment. All you need are new memory cards and a flat screen monitor to provide yourself with the modern conveniences of a brand new desktop.

The most obvious disadvantage of a desktop is the lack of mobility. People with laptops take them everywhere; they work from anywhere and everywhere. With a desktop, if you want to work on an assignment over your lunch hour at work, you cannot pick up and go to a quiet corner in a cafe or park to get away from your desk. You may be able to log in from your work computer remotely. However, you remain chained to your desk if you work on a desktop. Desktops have a few other disadvantages. However, none is as noteworthy as the lack of flexibility.

Laptops

Let us this question from another perspective. Which do you prefer, a desktop or laptop for online college? The greatest advantage of the laptop is the greatest weakness of the desktop. Mobility is worth every penny to most people. If you want to take your family on a vacation, while you have time off from work, you do not need to sit at home to ensure you turn in your assignments. You can take off for Florida and turn in your assignments from the hotel. A great disadvantage of the laptop is the cost. In addition, these mobile miracles are more likely to break or pilfered. At the end of the day, you must find a computer to meet your needs and budget.

The decision of a desktop or laptop for online college is yours. Today, a computer is available for all budgets. The most important point is to take online courses to enhance your career.
