A Laptop or a Full-Sized Computer: Choose Your Thing for College

February 16, 2011 by admin Leave a Comment

A college freshman needs to deal with many new things in the college, such as projects, assignments, research, deadlines, etc. As a freshman, it is quite helpful to have your own computer as it can assist you in completing your routine tasks very efficiently and conveniently. However, it is often difficult to decide whether you should buy a traditionaldesktop or a laptop, considering your own specific needs. To make a well-informed decision, it can be helpful to first analyze the purposes for which you need a computer and then to identify which of the two suits your needs better.

Here are few points you should consider while making a choice between a laptop and a desktop:

  • A laptop is smaller in size as compared to a desktop. Therefore, hard drives in a laptop usually have relatively smaller storage capacities than their desktop counterparts. So, if you need a computer for data storage, it is advisable to go for a desktop.
  • If you need a computer for research, for taking notes, or for any other purpose that demands mobility, buying a laptop will be the better choice. Not only a laptop is movable, but it can also work on battery power giving you the freedom to work wherever you want.
  • If your work involves graphic editing, animations, designing etc., choosing a desktop is advisable because of the larger screen sizes and flexibility of desktop monitors.
  • With a laptop, you get all the peripherals built into the machine, which need to be separately bought in case of a desktop. This may be either good or bad depending upon your choice of peripherals and budget.

When you are thinking about the items you should bring to college, your computer is a must-have on the list. However, the decision of which one to buy should be made based on your requirements as well as the economic feasibility of buying the most suitable solution.
