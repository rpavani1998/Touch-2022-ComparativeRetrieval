Laptops Vs. Desktops: Which is Right for You?



Buying a new computer is no easy task. Most of the time, it involves plenty of research to narrow down even ten computers that you’d be interested in owning. When you think about the amount of money and time that you’ll be investing into your new machine (because people normally keep computers for at least four years before moving on), it makes your decision all the more important.

When you look at your options, it usually comes down to whether you want a desktop or a laptop. Both have their advantages, so you’ll have to decide based on these strengths.

Laptops

Laptops are incredibly portable and come with batteries which allows users to do tasks away from a power source. They can be tucked a backpack, carried around, opened up, and they’re ready to go.

Pros:

-Super portable
-Battery allows you to do tasks away from a power outlet
-Relatively cheap, depending on the brand

Cons:

-More expensive to upgrade
-Not much room to upgrade, if any
-Can’t change screen size
-Battery will eventually need to be replaced after some years

As you can see, there are plenty of pros to owning a laptop, but there are also plenty of cons that may impact your buying decision. Of course, if you really don’t feel the need to upgrade your hardware or screen, and also don’t mind dishing out some cash for another battery, then a laptop could be perfect.

There are some who just cannot stand sitting in a chair without the option of being mobile, so a laptop offers exactly that.

Right now, there are some quality laptops being offered such as the:

  • Dell XPS 15 for only $799
  • HP Pavilion dv6t Quad Edition for only $830.99
  • Lenovo Thinkpad for only $699

Desktops

Thought the original big machine was extinct? Think again. The market for a quality desktop is still strong, and it doesn’t seem to be going anywhere.

Why would people buy a desktop when a laptop is much easier to manage, you say?
Pros:

-Cheaper than laptops
-Easy to upgrade, and plenty of room to do so
-Your choice of monitor

                Cons:

-Bulky
-You must sit down to use it
-Not portable

Most people who purchase desktops do so because it is cheaper and easier to build a custom computer that is capable of playing the most recent games and serious video and photo editing. With the ability to have a larger screen that any laptop, you can get more real estate to work with as well.

For instance, if you operated a photo editing studio, a laptop could certainly fit the bill, but you’d be able to do more graphic intensive work and be able to see what you’re doing better on a desktop.

It all comes down to preference.

Here are some quality desktops out there currently:

  • HP Pavilion HPE h8m Series for only $599.99
  • Dell XPS 8300 for only $699.99
  • Asus Essentio for only $629.99

Conclusion

That said, the decision to own a laptop or a desktop is really up to you. If you feel the need to upgrade hardware on a constant basis, go for a desktop. If you enjoy mobility, go for a laptop.

There will be people out there who will argue for either side, but none of them know what you want. You can readNew York Kia articles to see what kind of systems they recommend, or you can check out other computer websites for reviews on computers you’re looking for, and you still might not know what to pick.

Go with your gut and what you need!
