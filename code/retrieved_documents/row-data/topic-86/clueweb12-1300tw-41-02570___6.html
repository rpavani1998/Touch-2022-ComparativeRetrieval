Ten Ways to be a Better Baseball Player

Ten Ways to be a Better Baseball Player

  • Stay active in the off-season. Participate in other sports. Coaches want athletic players in general, and the more you focus on making yourself a better athlete, the more your baseball performance will naturally improve.
  • Warm up well before games. Don't look to throw the hardest or swing the fastest. Focus on your range of motion and activating your core muscles. The looser you feel before the game, the better you'll start out.
  • When you're on deck, don't just swing your bat around to warm up. Also take a look at what the pitcher is doing—what pitches they're choosing, how accurate they are, etc. Knowing what to expect can set you up to get a better hit.
  • Watch a pitcher throughout the game to observe how they react in pick-offs. How attentive are they to base runners? How aggressive at they? Knowing these tendencies can mean the difference between getting a steal, just hanging back or getting picked off for an out.
  • Don't waste your dugout time either. Watch the opposing team's outfield for weak links. If there's an opening or a player that isn't totally focused, that may be where you're aiming during your next at-bat.
  • When you're at the plate, check again for that gap in coverage. Hitting the right space can turn your single into a double.
  • When you're on base, pressure the defense. Even on a grounder, take off from your base. Make the fielders switch their focus to tag you.
  • Make the easy plays really look easy. Develop your skills with basic drills, knowing all along that it will not only help with the basics, but also with more advanced plays.
  • Be aggressive in the outfield. Don't wait for balls to reach your glove—go to them. Develop quicker feet so you can get to the play as quickly as possible.
  • Take criticism well. There's always room for improvement, and someone outside the play may be able to see things you can't. Never take it personally. If you're training to be your best, then why not take that advice?
