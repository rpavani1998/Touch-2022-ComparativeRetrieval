Have Your Milk and Drink It, Too!

Submitted by Jess Bigadza on Thu, 2010-10-07 08:57 in
  • Nutrition

SGE Review

There are always new developments and trends in healthy eating.  Today, people are drinking soy milk at higher rates than ever before.  Whether you are a vegan or just don’t like the taste of cow’s milk, read how switching to soy milk could effect your diet.

 

Full Article

Most Americans consume dairy at least two times per day.  This isn’t always the case for many who are lactose-intolerant, however.  Lactose intolerance is the inability to digest lactose, a sugar found in dairy products.  Common symptoms include severe digestive problems, which can lead to the elimination of milk products completely.  Luckily, there are many tasty alternatives, such as soy milk.  But is soy milk healthy for everyone?

 

 

 


The good

 

  • Soy milk is, in fact, a healthy option for lactose intolerant individuals. 
  • While lactose-free cow’s milk is available, it can still contain traces of lactic acid, up to 30 percent.  Instead of giving up milk completely, many people have switched to drinking soy milk, which is 100 percent lactose free.
  • It can also be healthy for those who can consume cow’s milk because it contains adequate levels of protein, calcium and vitamins that are necessary for a balanced diet.
  • Soy milk is also shown to contain more fiber than regular milk, which helps regulate digestion, prevent against colon cancer and keep weight under control.
  • Some studies have proven that soy milk can be a main factor in lowering blood cholesterol and the risk of heart disease.  There are a variety of flavors to try when switching to soy milk, including sweetened, unsweetened, chocolate, vanilla, coffee and many others. 

  

 

The bad

 

  • While drinking lactose-free alternatives, such as soy milk, can be a lifesaver for lactose-intolerant individuals and vegetarians, it is not always the best option for those who can tolerate the lactose in cow’s milk.
  • Many people make the assumption that drinking soy milk must be a healthy option because it lacks the hormones that can sometimes be found in cow’s milk.  However, consumers do not usually consider the amount of fat soy milk contains in comparison to cow’s milk.
  • One serving of soy milk can have at least 5 grams of fat per cup.  Also, soy milk can increase a woman’s estrogen level, which can cause fat to collect in various problem areas of the body, such as the hips or stomach.  The most common complaint about soy milk is the taste.
  • Soy milk has an acquired taste that can take time to get used to.  When switching to soy, it should be done gradually.  Other side effects of consuming soy milk include an increase in hot flashes among menopausal women, kidney stones and bloating.
