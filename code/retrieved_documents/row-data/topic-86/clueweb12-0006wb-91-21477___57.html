Collaboration Tools for the Research Commons

Proposal ID 2010-129-1
Permanent Link http://techfee.washington.edu/proposals/view/2010-129-1/
Department University Libraries
Non-core Access No restrictions
First Application? Yes

Abstract

This proposal requests funding for hardware and software in the UW Libraries Research Commons, currently being developed on the ground floor of Allen Library South and scheduled to open Autumn Quarter 2010. Made available to all students at the UW, the Research Commons is being designed primarily as a community of scholars for graduate and undergraduate students doing research. The space itself will be a media-rich collaborative learning environment in which students can make use of technology to work with others in a variety of ways. While we will be utilizing some equipment from the closure of the Mary Gates Hall Computer Resource Center (CRC) this summer, the Libraries’ vision for the Research Commons involves technology that does not currently exist in the CRC, and that is easily accessible to all students, flexible in purpose, and compatible with student lifestyles. The Commons facilities will include support for group research, seminars, presentations, and the creation of media productions through space and technology tools and equipment. We are requesting funding for large display screens that can be used collaboratively with students’ laptops, videoconferencing hardware and software that will allow students to meet virtually, equipment that will help students practice presentations, an easily-accessible large format scanner and a whiteboard capture system that will allow students to better save and share the spontaneous ideas recorded on a whiteboard.

Background

Currently, the Libraries offers a number of technology-rich spaces for student work. The Odegaard Learning Commons is a 360-workstation computing center open 24 hours a day during the week. Computing services available in the Suzzallo-Allen, Odegaard, Engineering, Foster Business, and other branch libraries have expanded over the years, giving students increased access to computer lab services and collaborative workstations. With the upcoming closure of the Mary Gates Hall Computing Resource Center and the HUB renovation scheduled to begin this summer, we recognize that technology-mediated group collaboration space will be in high demand for students in the next 2 years. The Libraries understands the growing importance of group study space, particularly as interdisciplinary research increases and coursework requires group collaboration. We have provided more group work space within the Libraries and partnered with Learning & Scholarly Technologies (LST) to provide Collaboration Studios, Videoconferencing facilities, and Digital Presentation Studios in the past. Along with the need for collaborative learning space, the Libraries recognizes the needs of researchers as they move toward a digital future. More than ever, support for students doing research needs to involve technology-mediated collaboration systems that allow for communicating, sharing, saving and learning about research data.

In the fall of 2010, the Libraries will open a Research Commons on the ground floor of the Allen Library. Planning for this space is the result of a proactive effort by the Libraries to address the need for collaborative, flexible space for study and teaching. As the UW Libraries strives to become an “Anytime, Anyplace” library through more and better virtual services and collections, we have been shifting from a focus on building physical (print) collections to a more dynamic place of discovery among our communities of students. A Research Commons Planning Committee was appointed in June 2009 to investigate how the space on the ground floor of Allen Library South could best be designed, and what services might be offered to address student learning space needs. Key stakeholders around campus were interviewed and focus groups of students were conducted to identify needed resources and services. The Undergraduate Libraries Student Advisory Council and Graduate and Professional Libraries Student Advisory Council were consulted for their input on learning spaces and services to support student research. The primary themes and needs that emerged from these conversations were: videoconferencing facilities, space and technology for collaborative work, meeting/presentation/display space as well as services for getting help with presentations, writing and new publication formats. The committee’s findings in terms of student learning space needs matched the findings of UW Learning & Scholarly Technology’s recent report, “Designing Campus Learning Spaces: A Report on Students’ Current and Future Needs.” In this report, in which survey and focus group data was gathered from over 3,250 UW students, recommendations were made to the UW community for establishing spaces for collaborative work and mixed-use space as well as providing high-end software, ease of laptop use and access to printing. Our STF proposal addresses the student needs expressed in both of these investigations.

One of the identified barriers to using the existing Collaboration Studios in Odegaard and Suzzallo is the need for students to download special software to their own computer. Providing screens that can easily be connected to student laptops is a more realistic solution for enabling technology-mediated group collaboration. The space will be designed for flexibility, with the largest square footage going to multipurpose group work areas. Individual workstations, provided from the MGH CRC, will take up a portion of the Research Commons, though the overall goal is to allow for multifunctional, technology-enhanced group collaboration space. We will be working with a design firm to make significant changes to the space as it is now, adding comfortable, attractive, colorful furnishings, lighting, and power outlets. We are excited for the potential that the Research Commons will offer to UW students, and are requesting funding for technology that will help us fulfill our mission to support and enhance student research in the Research Commons.

Benefits

Based on input gathered by the Research Commons committee from June-December of 2009, as well as data from Learning and Scholarly Technology’s report on campus learning space needs of students, we are proposing the following items that we strongly believe will improve student research and the learning experience.

Large plasma screens will provide students with the ability to work comfortably in groups, using their own laptops which they can connect via cable to the screen. Two of the eight screens requested will have dedicated computer workstations, eliminating the need for a laptop. The spaces in which these screens will exist will also include comfortable, flexible seating. Some screens will be placed in dedicated seating areas, but most will allow for spontaneous group gathering around a screen.

Mobile data projectors will allow for a presentation space in which students can practice their research presentations, as well as impromptu teaching space in which librarians, students, teaching assistants or other campus partners can conduct student workshops. We also envision using a mobile projector for evening events such as lightning round talks in which students can share their research while practicing public speaking, or small performances, talks or instruction sessions in an informal, comfortable environment.

Webcams, Microphones, and Speakers used with the large plasma screens will allow students to connect to others via video chat (such as Skype) or videoconferencing. Providing a simple, easily accessible way for students to connect with one another will benefit students by saving travel time and cost, as well as providing a more robust method of saving recorded conversations.

Adobe Connect software installed on machines would allow students to collaborate using an interface that includes video chats, media uploads and a drawing board. Students who wanted to meet with other students while at home could connect in the Research Commons by using their own laptop’s webcam. In the future this software will also allow for users with an iPhone to connect directly to others in the Research Commons, making it even more convenient for meeting virtually as a group.

A Whiteboard Capture system will benefit students by allowing them to capture an image of ideas shared on an existing whiteboard, and then share it as a digital file in multiple formats. This is a better option than students relying on using their own cell phone camera (which often results in a poor quality, small, static image) to take a picture of ideas written on a whiteboard.

A Camera and Microphone available for students to record themselves as they practice presentations will help students as they prepare for formal research presentations, job interviews, and other public speaking projects.

Large format Scanner with Document Feed: While scanners are available for checkout at the
Allen North Library Information Desk, it will save student time to use a large format desk scanner that utilizes a document feeder to quickly scan multiple pages.

Student Access

The Research Commons, as with all other public spaces in the UW Libraries, will be open to all UW students. Research Common’s quarterly hours of operation will be the same as that of the Suzzallo & Allen Libraries: M-Thu 7:30am-10pm, Fri 7:30am-6pm, Sat 12pm-5pm and Sun 1pm-10pm.

Available Resources

The Libraries provides funding for day-to-day operations and general administration of Libraries' workstations and equipment. Libraries Information Technology Services is responsible for developing and deploying software images, installation, and arranging for in-warranty hardware repair if required. A Libraries staff member will be available at all hours of operation. Libraries Information Technology Services and UW IT Learning and Scholarly Technologies will partner in supporting the technology in the Research Commons.

The office of the Provost is providing minor capital funding to upgrade the area including additional electrical, network and wireless capacity. In addition, funds are provided to assist with re-envisioning the space through the use of furniture, color and textures to meet the needs of students and complement the technology we hope to add. The total investment by the Provost is expected to be approximately $300,000.

Installation Timeline

Work to redesign and refurbish Allen Library South will begin during Spring Quarter 2010, and will be completed before the start of Fall Quarter 2010. Equipment described in this proposal will be ordered over the summer and installed when furnishings and power supply outlets are complete in the redesigned space.
